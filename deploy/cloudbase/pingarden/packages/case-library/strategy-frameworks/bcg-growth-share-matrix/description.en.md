# BCG Growth-Share Matrix

BCG Growth-Share Matrix is a classic portfolio-management framework that classifies businesses by **market growth** and **relative market share**. The four categories — Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, and Dogs — help leaders decide where to invest, harvest, select, divest, or reposition.

## When to use

Use it when a company has multiple businesses, brands, geographies, or product lines and the strategic question is about **resource allocation under market-growth and competitive-position assumptions**.

Do not use it when share data is unavailable, when the portfolio is mostly early innovation options, or when profit pressure comes from industry structure rather than portfolio position. In those cases, pair it with `business-model-environment`, `porters-five-forces`, or `portfolio-map`.

## How it maps to PinGarden canvases

- `bcg-growth-share-matrix` is the working 2×2 canvas. Each unit is placed as a sticky or pin-like decision object.
- `portfolio-map` is the broader umbrella view when risk, return, and innovation/disruption movement matter more than share.
- `business-model-canvas` expands any unit that receives an investment, harvesting, divestment, or repositioning decision.
- `business-model-environment` validates the market-growth assumption and market maturity signals.

## Workflow

1. Define comparable units: businesses, product lines, brands, or geographies.
2. Estimate market growth for each unit using category or segment data.
3. Estimate relative market share against the largest competitor or relevant leader.
4. Place each unit in Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, or Dogs.
5. Add evidence notes explaining the growth/share assumptions.
6. Choose actions: invest, harvest, select, divest, reposition, or recheck data.
7. Expand high-stakes decisions into BMC and environment analysis before committing resources.

## How to interpret results

- **Stars** need investment to defend leadership in a growing market.
- **Cash Cows** generate cash in slower-growth markets and can fund selected future bets.
- **Question Marks** need selective investment; only some should become Stars.
- **Dogs** require a decision: divest, harvest, reposition, or keep for a named strategic reason.

## What to notice in examples

- `nestle-portfolio` is the strongest example because mature categories and portfolio actions make the Cash Cow logic visible.
- `alibaba-group` shows how growth markets and leadership positions vary across commerce, logistics, cloud, and platforms.
- `ping-an-group` can be read as a mix of financial Cash Cows and higher-growth technology/platform bets.
- `procter-gamble-cd` shows why portfolio funding and external innovation selection need explicit resource logic.

## Common misuses

- Drawing the matrix without market-growth or relative-share evidence.
- Treating quadrant labels as automatic decisions.
- Funding all Question Marks equally because their markets grow.
- Harvesting Cash Cows so aggressively that the portfolio loses its funding engine.
- Keeping Dogs because of internal politics or team attachment.
- Ignoring profitability, defensibility, synergies, or business-model fit.
