# Business Model Portfolio Management

Business Model Portfolio Management helps a company manage two different jobs at the same time: exploiting existing business models and exploring future ones. The point is not to celebrate innovation activity. The point is to make the whole portfolio visible, compare risk and return, and decide what to grow, protect, improve, transfer, divest, or kill.

## Core idea

An invincible company constantly reinvents itself before it becomes obsolete. It keeps improving and growing current business models while also searching for new engines of growth. These two portfolios have different logic:

- **Explore portfolio** — innovation projects, new business models, new value propositions, products, and services. Plot them by **Expected Return** and **Innovation Risk**.
- **Exploit portfolio** — existing businesses, value propositions, products, and services. Plot them by **Return** and **Death & Disruption Risk**.

The Portfolio Map turns the conversation from isolated project opinions into a shared portfolio view.

## Management rhythm

Use the framework in three moves:

1. **Visualize** — list the existing businesses and future options at the same level of granularity. Place each item on the Portfolio Map.
2. **Analyze** — ask whether the portfolio is over-exposed to disruption, under-investing in the future, or carrying too many low-return / high-risk bets.
3. **Manage** — choose portfolio actions and make movement visible over time.

## Explore actions

Explore actions shape and test new business ideas to increase expected return and reduce innovation risk.

- **Ideate** — turn opportunities, technologies, or insights into first business-model prototypes.
- **Persevere** — continue testing based on encouraging evidence.
- **Pivot** — change the business model when evidence shows the current path will not work.
- **Retire** — kill a search project when evidence or strategic fit is weak.
- **Spinout** — move a promising idea outside the company rather than killing it.
- **Transfer** — move a de-risked model from Explore into Exploit.
- **Invest** — invest in an outside startup or exploration project to strengthen the portfolio.

## Exploit actions

Exploit actions manage existing business models to improve return and reduce disruption risk.

- **Acquire** — buy an outside business to add or strengthen a portfolio position.
- **Merge** — combine an acquired or owned business with existing businesses.
- **Partner** — strengthen a model through an outside partner.
- **Invest** — take a stake in an outside business to bolster the portfolio.
- **Improve** — renovate an outdated model into a more competitive one.
- **Divest** — sell or spin off a business that no longer fits.
- **Dismantle** — end and disintegrate a business.

## How it connects to canvases

- Use **Portfolio Map** as the main view.
- Use **Business Model Canvas** to expand any pin that needs deeper analysis.
- Use **Experiment Canvas** when an Explore pin needs evidence to reduce risk.

## Quality bar

A strong portfolio analysis names the unit of analysis, separates Explore from Exploit, places each item with a reason, and proposes actions. A weak analysis simply labels a company “innovative” without showing the portfolio logic.
