# Innovation Metrics

Innovation metrics answer one core question: is an Explore project actually reducing uncertainty, or merely consuming budget and producing activity?

## Core idea

Mature businesses can be managed with revenue, profit, growth, and efficiency metrics. Applying those too early to Explore work often forces teams to fake certainty. Innovation metrics focus on whether:

- critical assumptions are explicit;
- experiments produce real evidence;
- evidence strength justifies the next investment;
- risk is going down;
- the project should persevere, pivot, transfer, or retire on the Portfolio Map.

## Canvas connections

- Use `evidence-scorecard` to summarize the evidence state of one project or opportunity.
- Use `experiment-canvas` to design and review individual tests.
- Use `portfolio-map` to see risk/return movement across projects.
- Use `innovation-culture-map` to check whether the organization rewards real learning.

## Quality bar

Good innovation metrics do not encourage more activity; they improve decisions. Every metric should be able to change the next action: keep funding, change the assumption, run another test, transfer to a business unit, or stop.
