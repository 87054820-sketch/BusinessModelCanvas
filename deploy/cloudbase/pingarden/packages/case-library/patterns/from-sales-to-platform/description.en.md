# From Sales to Platform

> A company shifts from selling its own product or service to orchestrating a platform where others create value on top of it.

## Why this pattern matters

A sales model captures value one transaction at a time. A platform model can compound when developers, sellers, partners, or users make the system more valuable for each other. The shift is not just a new channel; it changes what the company must govern.

## What the shifted BMC looks like

- **Customer Segments** split into multiple sides: users, developers, sellers, partners, or enterprises.
- **Value Propositions** include access, tools, distribution, trust, or ecosystem leverage.
- **Channels** often become APIs, marketplaces, app stores, or partner portals.
- **Key Activities** move toward governance, ecosystem enablement, and platform reliability.
- **Revenue Streams** can combine subscriptions, commissions, usage, advertising, or ecosystem pull-through.

## Concrete examples

- `nvidia-cuda` — GPUs become a programmable accelerated-computing platform.
- `salesforce-crm` — CRM becomes a cloud platform and app ecosystem.
- `alibaba-group` — commerce services become a multi-marketplace ecosystem.

## What goes wrong

- **Platform without sides** — there is no real multi-party interaction.
- **Too much control** — partners cannot create enough independent value.
- **No governance** — quality and trust collapse.
- **Sales metrics only** — the company ignores ecosystem health.
