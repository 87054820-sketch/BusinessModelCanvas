# Reading map — Testing Business Ideas

This is the operating manual for `experiment-canvas` and `evidence-scorecard`. Read it after the team has a BMC, VPC, or new business idea and needs to turn “we believe” into “we will test.”

## What problem it solves

- Teams have many ideas but do not know which risk to test first.
- Experiments become marketing campaigns or research tasks without hypotheses and pass/fail criteria.
- Evidence strength is confused: interviews, clicks, pre-sales, and real usage are treated as equal.
- After results arrive, the team does not know whether to persevere, pivot, retest, narrow scope, or stop.

## Contents / reading map

1. **Design the experiment** — define hypothesis, risk type, target, evidence threshold, and decision rule.
2. **Test desirability** — whether customers really have the job, pain, interest, and buying motivation.
3. **Test feasibility** — whether the team can deliver technically, operationally, and legally.
4. **Test viability** — whether revenue, cost, channel, unit economics, and growth logic can work.
5. **Measure evidence** — choose by cost, time, and evidence strength, not only by ease.
6. **Decide next action** — connect results to persevere, pivot, run another test, narrow scope, or kill.

## How it maps to PinGarden

- `experiment-canvas` — the main place for hypothesis, experiment, metric, and decision.
- `value-proposition-canvas` — source of desirability hypotheses.
- `business-model-canvas` — source of viability hypotheses.
- `evidence-scorecard` — records evidence strength and risk reduction.

## What to read first

- **Unclear direction** — customer interview, online survey, discussion forums, search trend analysis.
- **Uncertain value proposition** — storyboard, clickable prototype, landing page, smoke test.
- **Uncertain delivery** — concierge, Wizard of Oz, manual service, single-feature prototype.
- **Uncertain willingness to pay** — letter of intent, pre-sale, crowdfunding, price test.

## Common misreadings

- **Running an experiment is not the same as validation.** Without pass/fail criteria, the team only collected information.
- **Positive feedback is not strong evidence.** Purchase, repeated use, and payment commitment are usually stronger.
- **Bigger experiments are not always better.** Early work should reduce the largest risk quickly, not build a mini product.

## Next steps in PinGarden

- Start validation with `experiment-canvas`.
- Track evidence quality with `evidence-scorecard`.
- Choose methods such as `customer-interview`, `clickable-prototype`, `smoke-test`, `wizard-of-oz`, and `pre-sale`.
