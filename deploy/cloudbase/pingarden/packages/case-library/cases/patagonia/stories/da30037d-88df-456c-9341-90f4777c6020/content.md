# Strategy lenses: McKinsey 7S and Porter Five Forces

Patagonia carries both **McKinsey 7S** and **Porter Five Forces** because its strategy is simultaneously an organizational-alignment case and an industry-pressure case.

The 7S reading starts with shared values: environmental activism is not a campaign added after the business model. It shapes strategy, product choices, supply-chain systems, hiring, communication style, and the skills the company develops around repair, materials, and advocacy. The case works because the soft elements and hard elements reinforce each other.

The Five Forces reading explains why that alignment matters competitively. Outdoor apparel faces rivalry, supplier pressure in responsible materials, buyer scrutiny, substitutes from cheaper apparel, and entrants that can copy sustainability language. Patagonia's response is not to escape the forces, but to change the basis of competition: trust, repair, durability, and mission reduce pure price comparison.

The transfer lesson is discipline. A purpose claim becomes strategy only when the 7S system supports it and when it changes how the firm handles industry forces. Otherwise it remains marketing language.
