# Bosch Accelerator explore portfolio journey

## Context and tension

Bosch is useful for understanding the Explore side of portfolio management. A mature industrial company needs future options, but large organizations often judge new ideas too early with execution logic: business plans, ROI forecasts, and fixed roadmaps. That does not reduce uncertainty; it turns uncertainty into theater.

The portfolio unit is an exploration project / team, not a business department. Each project should be located by innovation risk and expected return.

## Read the Portfolio Map

::canvas[portfolio-map]{}

The left side of the map shows an exploration funnel. Many early teams sit in the high-risk zone because they have little evidence beyond slides and assumptions. Retired teams are not traditional failures; they are low-cost learning. Persevering teams have enough evidence to test the next key assumption. Transfer candidates have reduced desirability, feasibility, profitability, and adaptability risk enough to deserve an owner in the business.

## Dynamic movement

| Project state | Starting point | Movement | Portfolio action | Decision standard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New idea | high-risk / low-evidence Explore | enter small experiment | Ideate / Invest | clear hypothesis and cheap test |
| Promising project | lower-risk Explore | continue the next experiment | Persevere | customer, technical, or business evidence |
| Conflicting evidence | stalled Explore | change direction | Pivot | which core assumption was invalidated |
| Weak evidence | low-return / high-risk Explore | stop | Retire | whether more funding only protects sunk cost |
| Transfer candidate | high-evidence / high-potential Explore | find business owner | Transfer | owner, budget, channel, and scaling path |

## Read the Evidence Scorecard

::canvas[evidence-scorecard]{}

The evidence scorecard makes the innovation metrics explicit. Bosch should not measure the accelerator by demo-day volume or number of ideas. The useful metric is whether each team is producing stronger evidence, reducing desirability / feasibility / viability risk, and earning a concrete portfolio decision: persevere, pivot, retire, or transfer.

## Culture Map implication

Culture must reward evidence and learning speed, not just polished plans. If managers punish retired ideas, teams hide bad evidence. If leaders require large ROI before cheap experiments, Explore work becomes innovation theater. The target behaviors are explicit hypotheses, fast tests, and honest retirement.

## Risks and limits

An accelerator can become theater: great demo days, little risk reduction. It can also fail at transfer because business units refuse to absorb the project. The Portfolio Map therefore needs experiment metrics and a transfer mechanism.

## Transfer lesson

Explore portfolio management makes failure cheap and learning visible. The goal is not to keep every idea alive; it is to produce a few credible future growth engines without betting the company too early.
