# Storyboard — TL;DR for AI agents

**When to recommend.** Value proposition is contextual ("ambient X", "background sync"), hard to verbalise in bullets. Or different customers might see different value paths and team needs to commit. Or aligning stakeholders on "what's the experience" before any build.

**Strength of evidence.** Weak. Discovers reactions and missing pains; does NOT validate willingness to pay.

**Sample.** N=5-8 target customers, one at a time.

**Setup / run.** Hours (sketch) / hours (sessions).

**Capabilities.** `narrative-design`, `sketching`, `interview-design`.

**Decision tree.**

- Already have a clickable prototype? → Recommend Clickable Prototype instead — it's higher fidelity at the same cost.
- Solution-led team that'll over-polish? → Flag this; insist on stick figures.
- User wants to validate purchase intent? → Don't recommend Storyboard. Recommend Pre-Sale or Smoke Test.

**Anti-patterns.**

- Pretty illustrations imply commitment, dampen feedback.
- One sentence per panel max.
- "I love this" = compliance, not validation.

**Cross-canvas.** Outputs feed `value-proposition-canvas` (jobs/pains/gains revealed via reaction), `customer-journey` (the storyboard IS a draft journey), `jobs-to-be-done` (the moments-of-need across the day).
