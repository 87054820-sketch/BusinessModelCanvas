# Boomerang

Hand a target customer a competitor's product (or a legacy alternative) and observe them using it cold. The delta between what they expected, what worked, and what frustrated them is a rich Discovery signal — at zero build cost on your side.

## When to use

- A direct or close competitor exists; users would otherwise solve the problem with that product.
- You want to map the *real* customer journey end-to-end, including the moments where the existing solution fails.
- You're sizing whether you can credibly differentiate; if users are perfectly happy with the competitor, your wedge isn't where you thought.
- You can't afford to build a prototype yet but interviews alone aren't surfacing concrete usage friction.

Don't use:

- No competitor or legacy substitute exists — there's nothing to boomerang.
- Your differentiation is an entirely new modality competitors don't address — observation of them won't reveal it.

## How to run

1. **Recruit 5-8 target customers** who haven't tried the competitor.
2. **Give them a real task to complete** end-to-end ("set up a recurring report with this tool"), not a feature tour.
3. **Observe silently for the first 10 minutes.** Don't help. Watch where they stop, re-read, switch tabs, give up.
4. **Debrief immediately** — what they expected, where they stalled, what they wished was different, whether they'd pay (then ignore the answer to that last one).
5. **Code the friction points.** A friction that 4-of-6 hit is a real wedge; a single-user idiosyncrasy isn't.

## What good looks like

- 4-of-6 hit the same 1-3 friction points.
- The friction points are downstream of capabilities you actually have or can build cheaply.
- Customers re-articulate the job/pain in their own words during debrief — your VPC right column gets sharper.
- At least one customer says "wait, why doesn't [competitor] just do X?" — that X is your potential wedge.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Demo-ing the competitor yourself instead of letting the user drive. You'll see your friction, not theirs.
- ❌ Treating "this is fine" as data — it's compliance. Probe for the last time they got stuck.
- ❌ Recruiting people who already use the competitor — they've adapted to its quirks; new users see the friction more clearly.
- ❌ Using results to "prove" your idea is better. Boomerang Discovers wedges; it doesn't Validate that you can win them.
