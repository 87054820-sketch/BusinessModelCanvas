# Concierge — TL;DR for AI agents

**When to recommend.** Riskiest unknown is whether the *outcome* is paid-worth, independent of tech. Or Smoke Test passed but suspect retention won't follow. Or domain expertise sits in user's head; question is "is this productisable?".

**Strength of evidence.** Strong (relative to other cheap experiments). Validates desirability + viability + feasibility-of-the-outcome simultaneously.

**Sample.** 3-7 PAYING customers (free invalidates viability), 4-8 week run.

**Setup / run.** Days (offer + ops playbook) / weeks.

**Capabilities.** `domain-expertise`, `customer-success`, `manual-operations`.

**Decision tree.**

- Product needs automation humans can't replicate? → DON'T recommend. Vertical slice.
- Free trial? → Push back; charge a token amount.
- Team has no domain depth? → Concierge fails — recommend Wizard of Oz instead (less domain dependent).
- Want to scale to 20+ customers? → Push back; that's a different test (early operations), not Concierge.

**Anti-patterns.**

- Free Concierge ≠ viability data.
- Scaling past 7 — ops collapse, learnings drown.
- Skipping the if-automated unit-economics math.

**Cross-canvas.** Validates `value-proposition-canvas` (delivered value, willingness to pay), `business-model-canvas` (revenue streams + cost structure post-automation), `customer-journey` (full delivery cycle including referrals).
