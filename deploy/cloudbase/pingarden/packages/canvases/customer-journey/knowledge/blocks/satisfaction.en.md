# Customer Satisfaction

The customer's emotional state at this stage of the journey. Pick one:
**happy** (😊), **neutral** (😐), or **frustrated** (☹️).

This row is the **emotional curve of the experience**. Read it across
the timeline as a line, not as a list. Where the line dips below
neutral is where redesign attention is most valuable; where it spikes
above is where you have advocacy energy to harvest.

## How to use the legend

The smiley legend in the left margin (😊 / 😐 / ☹️) is a deliberate
positional cue. Place a "happy" sticky visually high in the row, a
"neutral" sticky in the middle, a "frustrated" sticky low. When the
team steps back, the row's silhouette **is** the curve — they don't
need to read each sticky to see where the journey is failing.

## What goes on each sticky

- The valence (happy / neutral / frustrated), as a single emoji or
  word — the placement does most of the work.
- One sentence on **why** the customer feels this way, _in their
  words if you have them_. "Pricing page is opaque, has to email sales
  just to get a number" is more useful than "frustrated."
- Optional: the source. A direct quote from research is gold; tag the
  sticky so the next reader knows it isn't speculation.

## Per-stage examples

- **Discovery — frustrated:** "Pricing page is opaque, can't even
  estimate without sales."
- **Consideration — neutral:** "Demo answered the integration
  question, but I'm still doing my own due diligence."
- **Onboarding — happy:** "Got my first weekly report out before
  lunch — way faster than I expected."
- **Use — neutral:** "It works, but I still rebuild the same chart
  every Friday by hand because the export skips the column I need."
- **Renewal — frustrated:** "Finance is asking why we pay for two
  tools that look like they overlap. I don't have a one-pager I can
  forward."

## How this row feeds downstream

The valleys are the work. A "frustrated" sticky paired with a clear
need in the same column **is** a roadmap item — usually a high-leverage
one, because the customer has already articulated the cost.

When you walk the row left-to-right and the curve dips, the team's
question becomes: _"what would it take to lift this?"_ That question,
not the sticky itself, is the point of the canvas.

## Failure modes

- **All-happy curves.** A row of 😊 stickies almost always means the
  team is filling from imagination, not research. Real journeys have
  valleys; the canvas's job is to find them.
- **Aggregate emotions.** "Mostly fine, sometimes annoying" hides the
  signal. Pick one valence and explain when the others apply in a
  separate sticky if needed.
- **Forgetting to read across.** A scattered set of 5 emoji is just
  decoration. The value lives in the **shape**, so make sure someone
  literally traces the curve at the end of the workshop.
