# Customer Needs

The **functional job** the customer is trying to get done **at this
particular stage** of the journey. Stage-specific. Read horizontally
across the row and you should see the customer's job evolve as they
move through the experience.

A common test: if a need would fit in any of the five columns
unchanged, it's been written too generically. "Save time" is not a
need; "decide which of three vendors to call back without sitting
through a 30-minute demo" is.

## What goes here

- The functional outcome the customer is chasing **right now**.
- The constraint that bounds it ("…in under 15 minutes," "…without
  paging the team," "…before tomorrow's exec review").
- The "good enough" bar — what does the customer accept as
  done-for-this-stage?

## Per-stage examples

- **Discovery** — "Find a credible shortlist without sitting through
  vendor sales pitches."
- **Consideration** — "Confirm this thing won't break our integration
  with X before I escalate it to Procurement."
- **Onboarding** — "Get a real, non-toy report out by end of day one."
- **Use** — "Hand off the weekly summary to my boss in their words,
  with proof I can defend if challenged."
- **Renewal / Advocacy** — "Justify the next 12-month spend to a
  finance team that doesn't see the day-to-day value."

## How this row feeds downstream

This row is the **single highest-leverage input to the Value
Proposition Canvas** (see Pairs-with). Every customer need that
goes unmet today, especially when paired with a "frustrated" sticky
in the satisfaction row, becomes a Pain in VPC's customer profile.
Every need you serve well becomes a Gain.

## Failure modes

- **Generic needs** that fit every stage. Re-write with a stage-
  specific verb and a stage-specific constraint.
- **Solution-shaped needs** ("a Slack integration"). That's already a
  proposed answer; the need behind it is "find out about important
  events without checking another tab."
- **Aggregating multiple stages into one sticky.** One sticky per
  stage, even if the wording overlaps — the column is the
  information.
