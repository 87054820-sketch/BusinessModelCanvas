# Products and Services (产品和服务)

This is a list of what you offer. Think of it as everything the customer can see in your shop window. It includes all the products and services that help your customers perform functional, social, and emotional jobs or that help them satisfy basic needs.

Products and services do not create value on their own. They create value only when they are linked to a specific customer segment and when they address jobs, pains, and gains that matter to customers.

## Types of Offerings

### Tangible

> *Physical goods such as manufactured products, equipment, or merchandise.*

### Intangible

> *Services such as copyrights, after-sales support, consulting, or warranties.*

### Digital

> *Digital products such as music downloads, online recommendations, software, or mobile apps.*

### Financial

> *Financial products such as investment funds, insurance policies, or commercial financing services.*

## Relevance

Not all products and services are equally relevant to your customers. Some are essential to the value proposition, while others are merely nice to have. A good value proposition focuses on the offerings that matter most.

## Core Questions

- What are the products and services we offer to each Customer Segment?
- Which ones are essential and which are nice-to-have?
- Which products and services help customers perform functional, social, and emotional jobs?
- Which help customers satisfy basic needs?
- Which support jobs related to buying, co-creating, and transferring value?

## Examples

- **Tangible** ——「Ergonomic office chair with 10-year warranty」
- **Intangible** ——「24/7 technical support with 15-minute response time」
- **Digital** ——「Cloud-based project management tool with real-time collaboration」
- **Financial** ——「Revenue-based financing with no equity dilution」

## How it flows into VPC

Products and Services are the instruments you use to create value. They must be designed in response to what you have learned about Customer Jobs, Pains, and Gains. **A product without a clear link to a customer job is a solution looking for a problem.**
