# Pain Relievers (痛点缓释方案)

Pain Relievers describe how your products and services alleviate specific customer pains. They explicitly describe how you avoid or reduce the frustrations, annoyances, and risks that customers experience when getting a job done.

A good value proposition always focuses on the most extreme pains that matter to customers. You don't need to come up with a pain reliever for every pain identified in the customer profile—no value proposition can do that. Great value propositions focus on relieving the most extreme, limited set of pains.

## How Pain Relievers Create Value

### Save Time, Money, or Effort

> *Does your product or service help customers achieve savings? This could be about reducing time spent, lowering costs, or minimizing effort required.*

### Feel Better

> *Does your product or service make customers feel better? Can it eliminate frustrations, annoyances, and other headaches?*

### Fix Underperforming Solutions

> *Does your product or service fix underperforming solutions by introducing new features, achieving better performance, or improving quality?*

### Remove Difficulties and Challenges

> *Does your product or service remove difficulties and challenges? Can it make things easier or eliminate obstacles?*

### Eliminate Negative Social Consequences

> *Does your product or service eliminate negative social consequences? Can it help customers avoid losing face, power, trust, or status?*

### Eliminate Risks

> *Does your product or service eliminate risks? Can it address financial, social, or technical risks?*

### Restore Peace of Mind

> *Does your product or service help customers sleep better? By addressing major issues, reducing worries, or eliminating concerns.*

### Limit or Eradicate Common Mistakes

> *Does your product or service limit or eradicate common mistakes? By helping customers use the right solution.*

### Remove Barriers to Adoption

> *Does your product or service remove barriers to adoption? Can it eliminate upfront investment costs, make things easier to learn, or remove other obstacles?*

## Core Questions

- Which customer pains are we relieving?
- How are we eliminating or reducing negative outcomes, obstacles, and risks?
- Are we focusing on the most extreme pains?
- Which pain relievers are essential versus nice-to-have?

## Examples

- **Save time** ——「Automated expense reporting that reduces monthly close from 3 days to 30 minutes」
- **Feel better** ——「Real-time delivery tracking so customers never wonder where their order is」
- **Remove risk** ——「SOC-2 certified infrastructure with 99.99% uptime SLA」
- **Remove barriers** ——「Free trial with no credit card required and guided onboarding」

## How it flows into VPC

Pain Relievers answer the question: "How do we make the customer's life less painful?" They must directly address the most extreme pains identified in the customer profile. **A Pain Reliever that doesn't map to a real customer pain is just a feature, not value.**
