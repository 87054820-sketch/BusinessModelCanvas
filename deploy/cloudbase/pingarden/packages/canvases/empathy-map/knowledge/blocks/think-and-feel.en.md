# Think & Feel

What's going on inside their head — thoughts, emotions, worries,
aspirations. The internal monologue you can't directly observe; you
can only **infer** it from the outside circuit (See, Say, Do, Hear)
you've already filled in.

In Gray's updated canvas this quadrant sits **physically inside the
head** of the persona to enforce the rule: inferences about internal
state come AFTER you've collected external evidence, not before.

## Ask

- What occupies their mind throughout the day?
- What emotions are driving them right now?
- What are they quietly proud of? Quietly anxious about?
- What do they think but never say out loud?
- What would they admit only to a close friend?
- What does the gap between Say and Do tell us they secretly believe?

## Discipline

If a Think & Feel sticky doesn't trace back to evidence in **See, Say,
Do, or Hear**, it's a hypothesis — not a finding. Mark it with a "?",
treat it as something to verify in the next research round, and
revisit it when more data arrives.
