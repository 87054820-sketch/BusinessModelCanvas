# Explore (开拓)

The Explore portfolio is all about the SEARCH for new ideas, value propositions, and business models to ensure the future of your company. Exploration involves maximizing expected returns while minimizing innovation risk.

You improve expected return by working on your business model design. You decrease the risk of failure by testing and adapting your ideas iteratively until you find a value proposition that customers really want and a business model that can scale profitably.

## The Innovation Journey — Five Myths

### Myth #1: Find and execute the perfect idea

> *Reality: Ideas are easy but they are just a starting point. The hard part is to constantly test and adapt ideas until you have sufficient evidence they will work in reality.*

### Myth #2: Evidence will show a clear path forward

> *Reality: Innovation is about making informed decisions based on incomplete and potentially contradictory evidence. Sometimes killing an idea is the healthy thing to do.*

### Myth #3: A small number of big bets will lead to large returns

> *Reality: Exploration requires making a large number of small bets that you gradually reduce over time, based on evidence. Start by investing small amounts in many ideas, then double down on those with real evidence.*

### Myth #4: Exploration and exploitation require similar skills

> *Reality: They are two radically different professions. Exploration deals with high uncertainty and requires pattern recognition; exploitation requires planning and efficiency.*

### Myth #5: Innovation teams are renegades

> *Reality: Innovators need to be seen as partners who are essential for the future of the company. Otherwise they cannot access resources like clients, brand, and prototyping tools.*

## Portfolio Quadrants

![Potential Steps in the Exploration Journey — four quadrants based on expected return and innovation risk. Source: *The Invincible Company*](assets/pm-exploration-journey.png)

- **Rising Star** — Large financial potential + strong evidence of success
- **Promising Concept** — Large financial potential + weak evidence (needs testing)
- **Modest Improvement** — Low financial potential + strong evidence
- **Questionable** — Low financial potential + weak evidence (consider killing)

## Core Questions

- What new business models, value propositions, or products are we testing?
- How are we systematically reducing innovation risk through evidence?
- Are we making enough small bets, or are we relying on a few big ones?
- Do our explorers have access to the resources and credibility they need?

## Examples

- **Rising Star** ——「New SaaS product with 50 paying beta customers and 40% MoM growth」
- **Promising Concept** ——「AI feature prototype showing 3x productivity lift in pilot, but only 2 customers tested」
- **Kill** ——「IoT hardware project after 6 months of testing shows $200 customer acquisition cost vs. $30 lifetime value」

## How it flows into Portfolio Map

The goal is to systematically move projects from high-risk/low-evidence toward low-risk/high-evidence, and then transition the best ones into the Exploit portfolio. **Exploration is not about being right—it's about learning fast and cheap.**
