# Growth Rationale (增长理由)

The decision close-out zone. After candidates have been placed in their quadrants and compared, this is where the team writes the *commitment* — which quadrant has been chosen, what changes, and what is deliberately deferred.

## Core Questions

- Which quadrant has been chosen for this growth horizon? Why not the others?
- What BMC blocks must change to execute the chosen quadrant?
- What are the 12-month milestones — specific, dated, falsifiable?
- What is the riskiest assumption that will be tested first, with what experiment?
- Which other quadrants have been *explicitly deselected* and why?
- What is the capacity reservation — management attention, capital, team allocation?

## What good looks like

A growth rationale entry that another team member can read 6 months from now and understand:
1. Why this quadrant
2. What changed in the BMC
3. What was deferred and why
4. What evidence would trigger a re-evaluation

## Examples

- "Chosen: Product Development (men's apparel for existing women-styling customer base). Why not Diversification: brand stretch risk too high. BMC changes: Value Proposition (men's-fit algorithm), Key Activities (men's stylist hiring), Key Resources (men's inventory). Riskiest assumption: ≥25% of current female customers will gift or co-purchase for male partners. Milestone: 5,000 first-time male subscribers by Q4. Deselected: Market Development to Europe (currency + return-logistics risk too high in 12 months); Diversification into home goods (no adjacency)."

- "Chosen: Market Penetration (raise repeat purchase from 35% to 45% via loyalty + bundling). Why not Product Development: existing SKU has 18% untapped TAM. BMC changes: Customer Relationships (loyalty programme), Revenue Streams (tiered pricing). Riskiest assumption: tier-2 customers respond to 10% loyalty discount with ≥2× usage frequency. Deselected: Diversification (no adjacency identified yet); Market Development overseas (waiting for current-market saturation)."

## How it flows

Growth Rationale closes the Ansoff Matrix loop and triggers the next canvas: `business-model-canvas` to map the BMC changes, `design-criteria-canvas` to encode constraints (e.g., "must reuse existing channels"), and `experiment-canvas` to test the riskiest assumption. If the chosen quadrant is diversification AND the disruption hypothesis applies, run `disruption-diagnosis` next.
