# Market Development (市场开发)

Existing product × new market: take the same product to a new geography, new segment, or new use occasion. Medium risk — the product is proven, the customer is not. The riskiest assumption is whether the new market actually needs the product in the form you currently deliver it.

## Core moves

- **Geographic expansion** — same product in a new country or region.
- **Segment expansion** — same product targeted at a new buyer type (e.g., enterprise → SMB, B2C → B2B).
- **Use-occasion expansion** — reframe the product for a different moment of use (e.g., morning coffee → late-night work fuel).
- **Channel expansion** — reach new buyers through a previously untouched channel (e.g., D2C → marketplace; offline → digital).

## Core Questions

- Does the new market actually have the underlying job-to-be-done the product solves? Verified with research, not assumed.
- Does the product need *localisation* — language, regulation, payment, pricing, distribution — and how much?
- Who is the local competitor in the new market? Are they already serving this need?
- What is the unit economics in the new market — can the same product earn margin given new channel costs and pricing pressure?
- Is the *brand* known in the new market, or do you have to build it?

## Examples

- "Take subscription clothing styling from US women's apparel into UK women's apparel — language identical, regulation similar, but local fit + return logistics + competitor (ASOS) differ."
- "Move enterprise CRM into SMB segment via self-serve onboarding — same product, new buyer with different willingness-to-pay + support expectations."
- "Reframe energy drink from athlete recovery to office afternoon-fatigue — same product, new use occasion, new distribution (vending machines vs gym retail)."

## How it flows into BMC

Market Development changes BMC **Customer Segments** (a new segment is added), often **Channels** and **Customer Relationships** (different in the new market), and frequently **Revenue Streams** (different pricing power in the new market). Key Activities and Key Resources may not need to change — if they do, the move is probably product development, not market development.
