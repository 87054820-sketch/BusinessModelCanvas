# How to use

1. List demand side, supply side, and complementors first; do not only describe the platform owner.
2. Define the core interaction: who produces the value unit, who consumes it, and how the platform matches them.
3. Identify whether network effects are same-side, cross-side, or both.
4. Explain how governance protects quality, trust, and incentives.
5. Check whether monetization damages the core interaction.
6. Finish with cold-start path and major risks.

# Anti-patterns

- Claiming "network effects" without explaining their source.
- Forcing every participant into BMC customer segments and losing multi-sided logic.
- Looking only at growth while ignoring governance, trust, subsidies, and regulatory risk.
