# Service

Installation, repair, support, training, and post-sale customer care. The question: does service strengthen the customer relationship and feed back into upstream activities, or does it just absorb cost?

## Examples

- "Free in-store repair as a brand promise (Patagonia Worn Wear)."
- "24/7 technical support with SLA enforcement and incident-rate dashboards."
- "Onboarding + training as a paid service module separate from the core product."

## Flow

If Service is a differentiation source (brand-defining repair, white-glove onboarding), it must appear on the Value Proposition. If it is a cost centre with reactive incidents, look upstream for the root cause (often Operations or Marketing-Sales over-promising). Service quality is the most common test of whether the rest of the chain actually works.
