# Firm Infrastructure

General management, finance, planning, legal, corporate governance, and any centralised function the whole company relies on. The question: does the infrastructure layer enable — or constrain — every primary activity below?

## Examples

- "Global treasury and capital allocation with monthly portfolio review."
- "Centralised legal + IP team supporting product launches in 14 jurisdictions."
- "Strategic planning and corporate development with explicit BCG-style portfolio cadence."

## Flow

Infrastructure is invisible day-to-day but determines what the firm can actually do at scale. A multi-business company's connective tissue lives here. Document the rules and resources here that *every* SBU depends on; flag where they constrain a specific Ansoff or Three-Horizons move.
