# Operations

The transformation of inputs into outputs: production, assembly, testing, packaging, or — for services — the delivery process itself. The question: where is the cost or quality difference actually made? Operations is where most cost competition lives, but also where many differentiation strategies (quality, customisation, speed) live.

## Examples

- "High-mix flexible manufacturing with weekly schedule re-balancing."
- "24/7 cloud service operations with five 9s SLA across three regions."
- "Standardised consultative-sales playbook with quality scoring on every customer call."

## Flow

For each sub-activity, mark scale economics (yes / no), capability rarity (commodity / specialist / proprietary), and current capability strength (1-5). Differentiating operations should appear on the Value Proposition; cost-advantage operations should appear on the BMC Cost Structure and Key Resources.
