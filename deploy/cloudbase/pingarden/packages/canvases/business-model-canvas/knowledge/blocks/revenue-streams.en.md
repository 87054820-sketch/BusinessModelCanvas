# Revenue Streams (收入来源)

The Revenue Streams Building Block represents the cash a company generates from each Customer Segment. If customers comprise the heart of a business model, Revenue Streams are its arteries.

A company must ask itself, for what value is each Customer Segment truly willing to pay? Successfully answering that question allows a company to generate one or more Revenue Streams from each Customer Segment.

## Sub-categories / Revenue Types

### Asset Sale

> *The most widely understood Revenue Stream derives from selling ownership rights to a physical product. Amazon.com sells books, movies, and consumer electronics.*

### Usage Fee

> *This Revenue Stream is generated by the use of a particular service. The more a service is used, the more the customer pays. A telecom operator may charge customers for the number of minutes spent on the phone.*

### Subscription Fees

> *This Revenue Stream is generated by selling continuous access to a service. A gym sells its members monthly or yearly subscriptions in exchange for access to its exercise facilities.*

### Lending / Renting / Leasing

> *This Revenue Stream is created by temporarily granting someone the exclusive right to use a particular asset for a fixed period in return for a fee. For example, Zipcar.com rents out automobiles by the hour.*

### Licensing

> *This Revenue Stream is generated by giving customers permission to use protected intellectual property in exchange for licensing fees. Licensing allows right holders to generate revenues from their property.*

### Brokerage Fees

> *This Revenue Stream derives from services performed on behalf of two or more parties, such as credit card providers who charge retailers a percentage of the value of each sales transaction.*

### Advertising

> *This Revenue Stream results from fees for advertising a particular product, service, or brand. This is a common revenue source for media and event industries.*

## Pricing Mechanisms

- **Fixed Menu Pricing** — predefined prices based on static variables
- **Dynamic Pricing** — prices change based on market conditions

## Core Questions

- For what value are our customers really willing to pay?
- For what do they currently pay?
- How are they currently paying?
- How would they prefer to pay?
- How much does each Revenue Stream contribute to overall revenues?

## Examples

- **Subscription** ——「SaaS monthly plan at $29/seat with annual discount」
- **Usage Fee** ——「Cloud storage charged per GB per month」
- **Brokerage** ——「Marketplace takes 15% commission on each transaction」
- **Licensing** ——「API access tiered by request volume: 10K/mo free, then $0.001/call」

## How it flows into BMC

Revenue Streams validate whether your Value Proposition is commercially viable. They must align with what each Customer Segment is willing to pay for and how they prefer to pay. **A business model without a Revenue Stream is a hobby, not a business.**
