# Key Partners (重要合作)

The Key Partnerships Building Block describes the network of suppliers and partners that make the business model work. Companies forge partnerships for many reasons, and partnerships are becoming a cornerstone of many business models.

Companies create alliances to optimize their business models, reduce risk, or acquire resources.

## Sub-categories / Types

### Strategic Alliances between Non-Competitors

> *The most basic type of partnership or buyer-supplier relationship is designed to optimize the allocation of resources and activities. It's illogical for a company to own all resources or perform every activity by itself.*

### Coopetition: Strategic Partnerships between Competitors

> *Strategic partnerships between competitors—in the same industry but in different sectors—are known as coopetition. For example, an automobile manufacturer may work with a competitor to develop an electric vehicle platform.*

### Joint Ventures to Develop New Businesses

> *Joint ventures are created when two or more companies establish a new, independent enterprise. This is common when market opportunities are too complex or risky for a single company to pursue alone.*

### Buyer-Supplier Relationships to Assure Reliable Supplies

> *Many companies establish buyer-supplier relationships to assure reliable supplies. For example, a fashion retailer may work closely with a textile manufacturer to develop exclusive fabrics.*

## Core Questions

- Who are our Key Partners?
- Who are our key suppliers?
- Which Key Resources are we acquiring from partners?
- Which Key Activities do partners perform?

## Examples

- **Strategic Alliance** ——「Co-marketing partnership with complementary SaaS tool serving same ICP」
- **Coopetition** ——「Shared EV charging network with rival automakers」
- **Joint Venture** ——「50/50 JV with local distributor to enter Southeast Asian market」
- **Buyer-Supplier** ——「Exclusive supply agreement for organic cotton with certified farm cooperative」

## How it flows into BMC

Key Partners fill gaps in your own resources and activities. They should reduce risk, improve economics, or enable capabilities you can't build alone. **Not everything should be built in-house—partnerships are how you extend your business model beyond your own walls.**
