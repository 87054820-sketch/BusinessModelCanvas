# Key Resources (核心资源)

The Key Resources Building Block describes the most important assets required to make a business model work. Every business model requires Key Resources, which allow an enterprise to create and offer a Value Proposition, reach markets, maintain Customer Relationships, and earn revenues.

Different types of business models require different Key Resources. A microchip manufacturer requires capital-intensive production facilities, whereas a chip designer focuses more on human resources.

## Sub-categories / Types

### Physical

> *This category includes physical assets such as manufacturing facilities, buildings, vehicles, machines, systems, point-of-sale systems, and distribution networks. Retailers like Wal-Mart and Amazon rely heavily on physical resources.*

### Intellectual

> *This category includes brands, proprietary knowledge, patents and copyrights, partnerships, and customer databases. Microsoft and SAP depend heavily on software development, while media companies rely on content.*

### Human

> *Every enterprise requires human resources, but people are particularly crucial in certain business models. A pharmaceutical company, for example, relies heavily on human resources.*

### Financial

> *Some business models require financial resources and/or financial guarantees, such as cash, lines of credit, or a stock option pool for hiring key employees.*

## Core Questions

- What Key Resources do our Value Propositions require?
- Our Distribution Channels? Customer Relationships? Revenue Streams?
- What resources are most expensive?
- Which resources are hardest to replace?

## Examples

- **Physical** ——「Regional warehouse network with 2-day delivery coverage」
- **Intellectual** ——「Patent portfolio covering 47 granted patents in battery tech」
- **Human** ——「Core team of 3 senior AI researchers with 50+ combined publications」
- **Financial** ——「$5M revolving credit facility to manage seasonal inventory swings」

## How it flows into BMC

Key Resources enable everything on the left side of the canvas. They must directly support the Value Proposition in the center. **If you list resources that don't directly enable customer value, you're describing your company org chart, not your business model.**
