# Value Propositions (价值主张)

The Value Propositions Building Block describes the bundle of products and services that create value for a specific Customer Segment. It is the reason why customers turn to one company over another. It solves a customer problem or satisfies a customer need.

Value propositions may be quantitative (price, speed of service) or qualitative (design, customer experience). Some Value Propositions may be innovative and represent a new or disruptive offer. Others may be similar to existing market offers, but with added features and attributes.

## Sub-categories / Types

### Novelty

> *Satisfying an entirely new set of needs that customers previously didn't perceive because there was no similar offering. This often appears in technology-driven markets.*

### Performance

> *Improving product or service performance has traditionally been a common way to create value. Personal computers that offer faster processing speeds and larger storage capacity illustrate this.*

### Customization

> *Tailoring products and services to the specific needs of individual customers or Customer Segments creates value. Mass customization and customer co-creation are two approaches.*

### Design

> *Design can be an important element of a Value Proposition, particularly in the fashion and consumer electronics industries.*

### Brand / Status

> *Customers may find value in the simple act of using and displaying a specific brand. Wearing a Rolex watch signals wealth and achievement.*

### Price

> *Offering similar value at a lower price is a common way to satisfy the needs of price-sensitive Customer Segments. But low-price Value Propositions often require a fundamentally different business model.*

### Cost Reduction

> *Helping customers reduce costs is an important way to create value. Salesforce.com, for example, sells Customer Relationship Management (CRM) applications as a service via the Internet.*

### Risk Reduction

> *Customers value reducing the risks they incur when purchasing products or services. A one-year service guarantee on a new car is one way to reduce risk.*

### Accessibility

> *Making products and services available to customers who previously lacked access to them is another way to create value. NetJets, for instance, popularized the concept of fractional private jet ownership.*

### Convenience / Usability

> *Making things more convenient or easier to use can create substantial value. Apple, for example, has consistently made user-friendliness a central aspect of its products.*

## Core Questions

- What value do we deliver to the customer?
- Which one of our customer's problems are we helping to solve?
- Which customer needs are we satisfying?
- What bundles of products and services are we offering to each Customer Segment?

## Examples

- **Novelty** ——「First streaming service for live classical music concerts」
- **Customization** ——「Running shoes tailored to your foot biomechanics via app scan」
- **Cost Reduction** ——「Cloud CRM replacing expensive on-premise enterprise software」
- **Accessibility** ——「Micro-investment platform letting users invest spare change from daily purchases」

## How it flows into BMC

Value Propositions sit at the center of the canvas, bridging the right side (customer-facing value delivery) and the left side (internal value creation). Every activity, resource, and partnership on the left must directly support the promise you make to customers on the right. **Your value proposition is the contract you make with your customer—everything else exists to fulfill it.**
