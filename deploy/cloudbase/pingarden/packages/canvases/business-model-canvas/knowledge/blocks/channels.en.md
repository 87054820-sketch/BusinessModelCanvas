# Channels (渠道通路)

The Channels Building Block describes how a company communicates with and reaches its Customer Segments to deliver a Value Proposition. Communication, distribution, and sales Channels comprise a company's interface with customers.

Channels are customer touch points that play an important role in the customer experience. A company can choose between reaching its customers through its own Channels, through partner Channels, or through a mix of both.

## Sub-categories / Channel Phases

### Awareness

> *How do we raise awareness about our company's products and services? This includes advertising, public relations, social media, and word-of-mouth.*

### Evaluation

> *How do we help customers evaluate our organization's Value Proposition? This could include free trials, product demos, testimonials, or detailed specifications.*

### Purchase

> *How do we allow customers to purchase specific products and services? This includes physical stores, online shops, sales teams, and subscription sign-up flows.*

### Delivery

> *How do we deliver a Value Proposition to customers? This covers logistics, digital delivery, installation services, and onboarding.*

### After Sales

> *How do we provide post-purchase customer support? This includes help desks, warranty services, community forums, and return policies.*

## Core Questions

- Through which Channels do our Customer Segments want to be reached?
- How are we reaching them now?
- How are our Channels integrated?
- Which ones work best?
- Which ones are most cost-efficient?
- How are we integrating them with customer routines?

## Examples

- **Awareness** ——「Instagram influencer partnerships targeting Gen Z skincare enthusiasts」
- **Evaluation** ——「14-day free trial with guided onboarding walkthrough」
- **Purchase** ——「One-click checkout via mobile app with Apple Pay」
- **After Sales** ——「24/7 chat support with 2-minute first-response SLA」

## How it flows into BMC

Channels connect Value Propositions to Customer Segments. They must be aligned with the segments you serve: a luxury brand might rely on exclusive boutiques and personal relationships, while a mass-market product might need broad retail distribution and self-service digital channels. **The channel is the handshake between your promise and your customer.**
