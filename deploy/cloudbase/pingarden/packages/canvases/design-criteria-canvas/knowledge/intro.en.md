# When to use the Design Criteria Canvas

The Design Criteria Canvas turns vision, customer research, business goals, and constraints into clear standards for judging ideas. Use it after you have candidate concepts, value propositions, or business-model directions and need a shared way to decide which ones should move forward.

## Reach for it when

- The team needs alignment on what a good solution must satisfy.
- You have many ideas and need evaluation criteria before selection.
- Customer insights, vision, or strategy need to become actionable standards.
- Budget, time, technology, compliance, or infrastructure constraints shape the design.
- The team must explicitly name what it will not do.

## Skip it when

- You do not yet have a vision, problem, or candidate direction.
- You are validating one specific experiment — use the Experiment Canvas.
- You only want a feature list instead of design decision criteria.
