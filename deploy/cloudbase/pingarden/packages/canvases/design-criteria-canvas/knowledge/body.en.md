# Reading the canvas

The Design Criteria Canvas separates a team's decision standards into four categories: must, should, could, and won't. It borrows the spirit of MoSCoW prioritization, but the focus is not feature ranking; it defines what kind of solution is worth designing, testing, and investing in.

## The four criteria types

- **Must Haves**: non-negotiable criteria. Without them, the idea fails.
- **Should Haves**: important criteria that should be satisfied when possible.
- **Could Haves**: valuable extras that can wait until resources allow.
- **Won't Haves**: explicit boundaries that prevent wrong directions and scope creep.

## Recommended flow

1. Review the vision, customer research, validation results, business goals, and constraints.
2. Let each participant write criteria individually — one criterion per sticky.
3. Place each criterion into one of the four areas and explain why it belongs there.
4. Test every Must Have: would the project truly fail if this criterion were missing?
5. Merge duplicates, remove weak items, and rewrite vague criteria into clearer statements.
6. Turn the most important criteria into SMART standards: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound.

## Common mistakes

- Moving every “nice to have” into Must Haves until nothing is prioritized.
- Writing vague slogans like “great experience” instead of criteria you can judge.
- Listing only product features while ignoring business goals, experience principles, ethics, and operational constraints.
- Forgetting to update criteria as research and experiment results change.
