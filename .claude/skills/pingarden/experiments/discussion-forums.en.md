# Discussion Forums

> Passive listening on Reddit, Discord, Slack communities, Hacker News, Twitter, and niche industry forums where your target customer already hangs out. The cheapest discovery surface — text customers wrote unprompted is gold.

# Discussion Forums — TL;DR for AI agents

**When to recommend.** User can't access segment for interviews (no warm intros, no list), OR is new to the domain and needs vocabulary + landscape, OR wants to triangulate interview themes against unprompted text.

**Strength of evidence.** Weak. Confirms a pain *exists* in the wild, not how widespread or how monetisable.

**Sample.** Read 200+ posts; require 5+ independent posters across 2+ venues to call any theme a pattern.

**Setup / run.** Hours (pick venues, frame search queries) / days (200+ posts is a few sessions).

**Capabilities.** `community-research`, `qualitative-coding`, `search-querying`.

**Decision tree.**

- Segment is offline / regulated / private? → DON'T recommend forums. Suggest Customer Interview via warm intros + Online Survey.
- New to the domain, need vocabulary? → Forums first, then Customer Interview.
- Just need recruitment for interviews? → Forums double as a recruitment channel — flag this dual use.
- User wants quantitative weight or willingness-to-pay? → Forums DON'T deliver. Recommend Online Survey or Pre-Sale.

**Anti-patterns to flag.**

- One viral post = anecdote, not pattern. Below N=5 independent posters, no claim.
- Posting your own pitch to "see reactions" — that's Smoke Test territory; mixing modes corrupts the passive signal.
- Confusing complaints with willingness to pay.

**Cross-canvas.** Outputs feed `value-proposition-canvas` Pains/Gains, `empathy-map` Says/Thinks/Hears, `jobs-to-be-done` functional-job verbs.
