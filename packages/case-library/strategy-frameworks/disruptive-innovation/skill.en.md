# Disruptive Innovation

## Select this framework when

- A new entrant is *worse* than incumbents on conventional dimensions but better on a different one — predict whether incumbents will rationally ignore them.
- An incumbent is assessing whether to take a new entrant seriously; disruption theory says the danger is the entrant the dominant customer interview says to ignore.
- The team is sorting bets in an innovation portfolio into sustaining vs. low-end disruption vs. new-market disruption — each requires different metrics and organisation.

## Use these canvases

1. `business-model-canvas` — sketch incumbent BMC alongside disruptor BMC; the disruption is almost always a BMC-level move.
2. `portfolio-map` — disruptive bets sit in deep Explore; the foothold → mainstream → upmarket path is a pin migration.
3. `value-proposition-canvas` — for new-market disruption, customers are non-consumers; for low-end, customers are overshot mainstream.

## Questions to ask

- Who is the foothold customer (overshot or non-consumer)?
- On which dimension are we *worse*? On which *acceptable*?
- Why will incumbents rationally ignore? (Thin margin + cannibalisation risk.)
- What is the upmarket migration path?
- What are the riskiest assumptions: foothold willingness, improvement trajectory, incumbent inattention duration?

## Strict test (Christensen 2015)

Apply the label *only* if ALL three hold:
1. The entrant starts in a low-end or non-consumption foothold.
2. The entrant's performance is *initially inferior* on mainstream dimensions.
3. The entrant improves along mainstream dimensions over time while preserving the foothold advantage.

If any fail, what you have is a sustaining innovation, a new-market entry, or a direct competitor — all valid moves, but not "disruption".

## Hand-off to other frameworks

- For time-layering, position the disruption candidate as an **H3** bet in **Three Horizons**.
- For measurement, validate with **Innovation Metrics** before scaling.
- For portfolio decisions, place on the **Portfolio Map** via **Business Model Portfolio Management** — deep Explore quadrant.

## Red flags

Do not call every startup disruptive. Do not confuse new technology with disruptive innovation. Do not skip the foothold test. Do not put a disruptive bet under the incumbent's standard metrics — Christensen's organisational prescription is to insulate it.
