# Scenario Planning

Scenario planning extends Business Model Environment work: instead of listing external opportunities and threats, it builds several plausible futures around critical uncertainties and compares whether strategic choices still hold.

## Core idea

Single forecasts push teams to bet too early on one future. Scenario planning asks teams to identify driving forces, isolate the uncertainties that would actually change strategic choices, and combine them into several distinct but plausible scenarios.

## Canvas connections

- Use `business-model-environment` to collect trend, market, industry, and macro signals.
- Use `scenario-matrix` to turn two critical uncertainties into four scenarios.
- Use `business-model-canvas` to inspect pressure on the current model.
- Use `portfolio-map` to compare which bets survive multiple futures.
- Use `design-criteria-canvas` to turn robust moves into design criteria.

## Quality bar

Good scenarios are not optimistic/base/pessimistic forecasts. They force the team to see different strategic logics. The output should say which options to preserve, which signals to monitor, and which moves are worth making now.
