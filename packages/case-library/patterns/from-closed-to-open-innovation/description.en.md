# From Closed to Open Innovation

> Mature companies can shift from a closed innovation logic to an open one when internal R&D is no longer enough to keep the business model competitive.

## Why this pattern matters

Closed innovation assumes the best ideas should be invented, developed, and commercialized inside the firm. That logic breaks down when useful knowledge sits outside the company, when startups move faster, or when internal R&D produces assets that the core business will never commercialize.

This shift pattern reframes open innovation as a business-model move. The firm changes how it sources ideas, which partners matter, how intellectual property flows, and how internal teams evaluate external ideas.

## What the shifted BMC looks like

- **Key Partners** expand to include scouts, platforms, universities, startups, licensees, or patent pools.
- **Key Resources** include external knowledge and access rights, not only internal patents.
- **Key Activities** add scouting, integration, licensing, or ecosystem orchestration.
- **Cost Structure** shifts from only internal R&D spend to search, legal, and integration cost.
- **Revenue Streams** may include new licensing, spin-out, or shared commercialization income.

## Concrete examples

- `procter-gamble-cd` — outside-in innovation through Connect & Develop.
- `innocentive` — an intermediary platform for open problem solving.
- `glaxosmithkline-patent-pool` — inside-out release of idle or underused IP.

## What goes wrong

- **Not-invented-here resistance** — internal teams reject external ideas.
- **No integration capability** — good ideas arrive but cannot enter product pipelines.
- **IP confusion** — unclear ownership blocks collaboration.
- **Token openness** — the company launches a portal but does not change decision rights.
