# From Transactional to Recurring Revenue

> A company shifts from one-off transactions toward recurring revenue, subscriptions, replenishment, or usage cycles that make demand more predictable.

## Why this pattern matters

Transactional revenue forces the company to win the next sale again and again. Recurring revenue changes the rhythm: acquisition may be more expensive, but retention, usage, and lifetime value become the core economic questions.

## What the shifted BMC looks like

- **Customer Relationships** move toward ongoing engagement, onboarding, and retention.
- **Revenue Streams** shift to subscriptions, usage cycles, replenishment, or recurring contracts.
- **Channels** support renewal and continuous access.
- **Key Activities** include service reliability, personalization, support, and churn reduction.
- **Cost Structure** often increases upfront acquisition or service costs but improves predictability.

## Concrete examples

- `spotify` — music access shifts from ownership/transactions toward subscription and engagement.
- `salesforce-crm` — enterprise software shifts from on-premise purchase to recurring cloud subscription.
- `nespresso` — machine sale creates recurring capsule demand.

## What goes wrong

- **Subscription without habit** — customers cancel after the first cycle.
- **Bad unit economics** — acquisition cost exceeds lifetime value.
- **No retention engine** — the model sells once but does not create ongoing use.
- **Forced recurrence** — recurring billing annoys customers when value is not recurring.
