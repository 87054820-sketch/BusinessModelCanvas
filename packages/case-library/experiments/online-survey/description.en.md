# Online Survey

A quantitative survey of 100-1000+ target customers, run after Customer Interview rounds have surfaced themes worth measuring at scale. You're asking "how widespread is this pain across the segment?" and "which pain dominates?" — not "do you have this pain?" (interviews answered that).

## When to use

- After 5-12 Customer Interviews have surfaced 2-3 candidate themes; you want to know which is the dominant pain across the segment.
- You need a relative ranking — pain X vs Y vs Z — to choose what to build first.
- The segment is large and reachable through a list (existing customers, mailing list, paid panel, social).

Don't use:

- Before any qualitative work. You'll ask the wrong questions and produce confident-but-wrong answers.
- For pricing or willingness-to-pay claims — surveys overstate buying intent by 2-3×. Use Pre-Sale / Letter of Intent.
- When you can't get N≥100 responses from real targets (random panel ≠ your segment).

## How to run

1. **One outcome per question.** "How often do you do X?" not "How often and how painful is X?".
2. **Rank, don't yes/no.** "Rank these 5 pains from most painful to least" produces information; "Do you find X painful?" produces compliance.
3. **Anchor with specifics, not opinions.** "In the last 30 days, how many times did you…" beats "Generally, do you find it hard to…".
4. **Keep it ≤ 8 minutes.** Beyond that, completion drops sharply and answer quality degrades for the back half.
5. **Pilot with 10 before sending to 1000.** Half the questions you wrote will be ambiguous in ways you can't see.
6. **Segment in the analysis.** Don't average across early adopters and laggards — the average is no one.

## What good looks like

- N ≥ 100 from your actual segment (not a random panel).
- A clear winner: 60%+ rank pain X above pains Y and Z.
- Quotes from open-text fields that match the interview-stage language.
- An honest "we asked the wrong question" outcome on at least one block — that's the value of the pilot.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Skipping interviews and going straight to a survey. You'll measure something, just not what matters.
- ❌ Leading questions ("How frustrating is the X problem?") — collects compliance.
- ❌ Treating "would you pay $50/mo for this" as data. It isn't. Strip pricing questions out and run a Pre-Sale instead.
- ❌ Long surveys (30+ questions). Completion < 20%, the 20% is unrepresentative.
- ❌ "We got 12 responses, and 10 said yes" — N=12 is not a survey, it's a small interview round you didn't run face-to-face. Lose the percentages.
