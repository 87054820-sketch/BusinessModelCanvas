# Smoke Test

A real-looking landing page promoting a product or feature you have NOT built. Drive targeted traffic to it, count how many people **take an action** (sign up, "Get pricing", join the waitlist, click "Buy"). The first Validation move when you've Discovered something promising and need quantitative demand evidence before committing engineering.

## When to use

- Discovery is done — interviews + survey + forums all point to the same underserved need.
- You need stronger evidence than "people said they'd want this" before committing to build.
- The team has copy + design + traffic-acquisition skills (paid ads or organic seed).
- Decision gate: "do we get the engineering team to build this?" — Smoke Test is the right gate.

Don't use:

- No Discovery done yet — you'll smoke-test the wrong promise.
- B2B with long, relationship-driven sales cycles (a landing page won't surface real intent). Use Letter of Intent instead.
- Solution requires explanation that doesn't fit in 30 seconds of scrolling. Use Concierge or Wizard of Oz.

## How to run

1. **Single, sharp value proposition.** One headline, one subhead, one CTA. The page is testing a *promise*, not the product.
2. **Real-looking detail.** Pricing (even tentative), screenshots (mocked-up is fine but specific), social proof if you can earn it.
3. **One CTA.** "Get early access" / "Join waitlist" / "Get pricing". Multiple CTAs muddy the data.
4. **Drive 500-2000 targeted visits.** Paid ads to your ICP, an HN/PH post, a partner newsletter blast. Untargeted traffic is noise.
5. **Honor the promise.** Capture emails and tell signers honestly the product is in development; offer a slot for follow-up. Burning trust here poisons later experiments.
6. **Define your conversion threshold BEFORE running** — e.g. "≥ 8% of targeted visitors sign up validates the test". Adjusting after the fact = no test.

## What good looks like

- 500-2000 targeted visits.
- A clear conversion rate against your pre-set threshold.
- The path that converted (which ad → which copy variant) is identifiable, so you can replicate.
- A clean signup list of people who said yes — they become Customer Interview / Concierge recruits.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Fake the product. Don't show a checkout that takes a card and silently fails. Honest "we're building it, want early access?" is required.
- ❌ Untargeted traffic. PH front page brings curiosity-clickers, not your segment.
- ❌ Adjust the conversion threshold after the fact to declare success.
- ❌ Treat the email list as validated demand without a follow-up step. Many people sign up to "see what happens" and never convert to paying customers.
