# Discussion Forums

Passive observation in places your target customers already discuss their pains, jobs, and gains — Reddit, Discord, Slack communities, Hacker News, Twitter / X, niche industry forums. The cheapest Discovery substrate, especially when you can't yet recruit interviewees.

## When to use

- You can't access the segment for interviews yet (no warm intros, no list).
- You want to triangulate interview themes against unprompted text — the language customers use without being asked.
- You're new to the domain and need to learn the vocabulary, the heroes, the recurring complaints, the in-group jokes — before you ever pitch.
- The segment lives mostly in identifiable online spaces (devs on HN/Reddit, marketers on Twitter, ops on Slack communities, gamers on Discord).

Don't use:

- The segment is offline / private (regulated B2B, internal enterprise users) — there's nothing to listen to.
- You need quantitative weight on a pain — forums skew toward whoever shouts loudest.
- Single isolated complaints — one Reddit post is not a pattern.

## How to run

1. **Pick 3-5 venues** where your segment hangs out. Quality > breadth.
2. **Read 200+ posts** before you form any opinion. The first 50 will be unrepresentative.
3. **Search for problem language, not solution language.** "Hate when...", "any tool that...", "wish I could...", "anyone else struggle with...".
4. **Code the themes.** Tag each relevant post with the pain/job it expresses; count occurrences. Below 5 separate posters per theme, drop it.
5. **Quote verbatim.** Capture the original phrasing — it's your VPC sticky text and your marketing copy later.
6. **Interview the most articulate posters.** A high-signal Reddit thread is a recruitment goldmine — the OP often agrees to a 30-min call.

## What good looks like

- 5+ independent posters across 2+ venues raised the same theme unprompted.
- You can quote 2-3 representative lines per theme verbatim.
- You discovered at least one job/pain you weren't expecting — evidence the listening was real, not confirmatory.
- 1-2 posters became Customer Interview recruits.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Treating one viral Hacker News post as data. It's an anecdote.
- ❌ Posting your own pitch to "see what people say" — that's an Asana-Of-Oz / Smoke Test, not Forum listening; mixing them ruins the passive signal.
- ❌ Cherry-picking the posts that confirm your idea. Track all relevant posts, including the ones that contradict.
- ❌ Confusing "people complain about X" with "people would pay to fix X". Forums are great at the first, terrible at the second.
