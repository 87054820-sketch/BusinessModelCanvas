# Letter of Intent — TL;DR for AI agents

**When to recommend.** B2B, ACV ≥ $5k, need procurement-grade signal before committing engineering. Or settling between two ICPs. Or build is 3-12 months and need signed proof beyond verbal interest.

**Strength of evidence.** Strong (in B2B). Validates viability with real procurement constraints + actual budget vs vapor budget.

**Sample.** 3+ signed LOIs from economic buyers (not just users/champions). Pitch 8-15 to land 3.

**Setup / run.** Days (LOI draft + ICP list) / weeks (pitching cycle).

**Capabilities.** `b2b-sales`, `stakeholder-mapping`, `contract-drafting`.

**Decision tree.**

- B2C? → DON'T recommend. Pre-Sale.
- ACV < $5k? → Procurement overhead exceeds data value. Smoke Test + Concierge.
- No Discovery yet? → DON'T recommend. Pitch will mismatch buyer framing.
- Can't deliver within stated timeframe? → DON'T pitch — burns trust.

**Anti-patterns.**

- Pitching only users/champions ≠ pitching the buying committee.
- Treating LOI as a sale (~30-50% walk when real contract appears).
- Vague conditions ("we'd consider X") = yes you never collect on.

**Cross-canvas.** Validates `business-model-canvas` (revenue streams price + customer relationships type + channels of B2B sale), `value-proposition-canvas` (which value drove the signed offer).
