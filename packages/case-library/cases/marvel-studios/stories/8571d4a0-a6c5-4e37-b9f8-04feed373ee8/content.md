# Marvel Studios: from comic properties to a connected cinematic universe

## Context and tension

Before the cinematic universe, comic properties were often licensed as isolated films. The industry bet on famous heroes, star power, and one-off box office performance. Marvel had gone through bankruptcy and did not control all of its best-known characters. The red ocean was not kind to a weaker rights holder.

This is why the case belongs in the Blue Ocean Strategy library. The company did not merely improve within the old rules. It changed which buyers mattered, which factors deserved investment, and which inherited assumptions could be removed. A newcomer should read the case as a market-reconstruction story rather than a normal product-success story.

## The strategic move

Marvel Studios built a connected universe around characters it could control, using continuity, post-credit hooks, and serialized emotional investment. It turned individual films into episodes in a larger platform-like story world.

The strategic move combines differentiation with a cost or complexity break. The company raises a few factors that matter strongly to overlooked buyers, while reducing or eliminating factors that incumbents treat as mandatory. That asymmetry is what makes the move teachable: the curve is not higher everywhere; it is meaningfully different.

## Then read the Strategy Canvas

::canvas[blue-ocean-strategy-canvas]{canvasId="d811b7e0-1d8c-44dd-863e-6121c24f6c48"}

The Strategy Canvas should be read as a shape. The red-ocean baseline scores higher on legacy complexity and expertise-heavy factors. Marvel Studios scores higher on access, usability, personalized value, cost efficiency, or new-demand creation, depending on the case. That shape is the strategic argument. If the company tried to score high on every traditional factor and every new factor, it would likely recreate the cost structure of the old industry.

## ERRC logic

- **Eliminate:** dependence on only the most famous licensed characters.
- **Reduce:** one-off film logic and fragmented creative control.
- **Raise:** continuity, fan engagement, character arcs, and cross-film anticipation.
- **Create:** a connected cinematic universe that makes each film market the next.

## Risks and limits

Universe logic can become complexity. New viewers may feel locked out, creative fatigue can grow, and the model depends on disciplined portfolio sequencing.

The case therefore should not be copied mechanically. The transferable insight is the discipline of fit: the offer, channel, operations, revenue model, and cost model must all support the same value curve.

## Transfer lesson

A blue ocean can emerge when a firm turns isolated assets into a system where each release increases the value of the whole portfolio.
