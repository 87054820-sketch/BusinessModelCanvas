# Connect & Develop as an explore pipeline

## Context and tension

The original P&G story explains Open Business Models: how P&G shifted from “all good ideas must be invented internally” toward outside-in innovation. This companion story reads the same case as portfolio management.

The portfolio unit is not an individual product idea; it is an innovation source / transfer system. P&G's Exploit core is brands, retail distribution, manufacturing, marketing, and consumer insight. The tension is that this core is powerful, but internal R&D alone cannot generate enough future options with enough speed and variety.

The core brands on the right are the exploit engine: they already have revenue, shelf space, supply chains, and consumer memory. Closed R&D is also on the right, but marked as a risk watch item because it still produces value while no longer being sufficient as the sole source of future options.

External technologies and partner networks sit on the Explore side. They should not be evaluated with mature-brand ROI too early. The early questions are different: Is the technology feasible? Does it solve a consumer pain? Can P&G manufacture, brand, legalize, and distribute it?

## Dynamic movement

| Item | Starting point | Movement | Portfolio action | Decision standard |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core brands | high-return Exploit | keep exploiting | Improve / Protect | preserve brand, channel, and manufacturing advantage |
| Closed R&D | Exploit with rising risk | no longer sole source | Improve | keep it, but complement it with external search |
| External technologies | high-risk Explore | reduce risk through technical and consumer evidence | Ideate / Invest / Persevere | feasibility, manufacturability, consumer acceptance |
| Partner network | Explore search system | expand the option pool | Partner / Invest | whether it reliably brings high-quality opportunities |
| Validated ideas | moving from Explore toward Transfer | enter brand and manufacturing systems | Transfer | enough evidence to be absorbed by brand, supply chain, and channel |

## Mechanism

Connect & Develop is not “buying outside ideas.” It turns the external world into a repeatable exploration pipeline. P&G uses technology entrepreneurs, InnoCentive, YourEncore, and other bridges to widen the search space; then the company's brands, manufacturing, and channels absorb ideas that have enough evidence.

That is why the open-innovation story and the portfolio-management story complement each other. Open innovation explains why outside ideas can enter the company. The Portfolio Map explains how those ideas move from uncertainty toward scalable exploitation.

## Culture Map implication

The real blocker is culture. Closed R&D rewards “we invented it here.” Open innovation requires new behaviors: scouting outside, admitting that good ideas may come from elsewhere, co-creating with partners, and not rejecting ideas because they are “not invented here.”

Enablers include technology entrepreneurs, partner platforms, legal and IP integration capability, and leadership metrics that track externally sourced innovation. Without those cultural and governance mechanisms, the Explore pipeline will not consistently transfer into the Exploit core.

## Transfer lesson

For a mature company, portfolio management is not finished by launching an innovation department. The critical design is a repeatable transfer mechanism: outside options enter Explore, evidence moves them toward Transfer, and brands, supply chains, and channels complete the Exploit move.
