# Strategy lenses: Three Horizons, BCG, and McKinsey 7S

P&G Connect + Develop is not only an open-innovation story. It demonstrates how an incumbent manages future options, allocates attention across a portfolio, and realigns the organization around external innovation.

In a **Three Horizons** reading, the existing consumer-goods core is H1. External ideas, licensing, and partner technologies are H3 options until they show customer, technical, and commercial evidence. When a partner solution can be integrated into a brand platform and scaled through P&G's channels, it becomes an H2 growth engine. The important movement is not “open innovation” as a slogan; it is the migration from outside option to internal growth platform.

The **BCG Growth-Share** lens asks which mature brands can fund external-search capability and which new concepts deserve scale. Cash cows should not fund every idea; they should fund a portfolio of selected question marks that can become stars under P&G's distribution and brand capabilities.

The **McKinsey 7S** challenge is organizational. Strategy shifts from invent-everything-inside to connect-and-develop. Structure creates external interfaces. Systems screen, contract, and scale partner ideas. Shared values treat external inventions as legitimate. Skills, style, and staff must reward collaboration rather than internal ownership alone.

Open innovation becomes strategic only when horizon management, portfolio allocation, and organizational alignment reinforce each other.
