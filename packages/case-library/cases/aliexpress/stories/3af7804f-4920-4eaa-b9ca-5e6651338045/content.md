# Strategy lens: Porter Five Forces

AliExpress is a **Porter Five Forces** case because cross-border marketplace strategy is shaped by pressure from multiple sides, not only by rivalry with other e-commerce sites.

Industry rivalry comes from global marketplaces, local champions, and category specialists all competing for sellers, traffic, and trust. Buyer power is high because consumers can compare prices instantly and switch platforms with low cost. Supplier power sits with merchants, logistics providers, payment rails, and traffic channels. Substitutes include local retail, brand-direct stores, social commerce, and super-app commerce. New entrants are difficult at full platform scale, but niche cross-border specialists can attack profitable categories.

The case teaches that marketplace advantage is a system: seller density, price discovery, logistics, buyer protection, payments, and dispute handling reduce the force pressure together. If any one layer weakens, buyer power and substitute pressure rise quickly.

Use Five Forces to locate where pressure enters the BMC, then use the platform ecosystem map to show how governance and trust mechanisms keep the core interaction working.
