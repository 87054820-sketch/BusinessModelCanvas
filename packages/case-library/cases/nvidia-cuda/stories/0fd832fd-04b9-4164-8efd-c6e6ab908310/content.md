# NVIDIA CUDA: turning GPUs from graphics chips into a computing platform

## Context and tension

GPU competition originally centered on graphics performance for gaming and visualization. CPU-centric computing treated GPUs as specialized peripherals. Many workloads, however, needed parallel computation that CPUs handled inefficiently.

This is why the case belongs in the Blue Ocean Strategy library. The company did not merely improve within the old rules. It changed which buyers mattered, which factors deserved investment, and which inherited assumptions could be removed. A newcomer should read the case as a market-reconstruction story rather than a normal product-success story.

## The strategic move

NVIDIA built CUDA to let developers program GPUs for general parallel computing. It reduced dependence on the gaming-only graphics cycle and raised developer accessibility, libraries, and ecosystem lock-in.

The strategic move combines differentiation with a cost or complexity break. The company raises a few factors that matter strongly to overlooked buyers, while reducing or eliminating factors that incumbents treat as mandatory. That asymmetry is what makes the move teachable: the curve is not higher everywhere; it is meaningfully different.

## Then read the Strategy Canvas

::canvas[blue-ocean-strategy-canvas]{canvasId="78b21bff-7528-48da-a69a-12d2e530c970"}

The Strategy Canvas should be read as a shape. The red-ocean baseline scores higher on legacy complexity and expertise-heavy factors. NVIDIA CUDA scores higher on access, usability, personalized value, cost efficiency, or new-demand creation, depending on the case. That shape is the strategic argument. If the company tried to score high on every traditional factor and every new factor, it would likely recreate the cost structure of the old industry.

## ERRC logic

- **Eliminate:** the boundary that GPUs are only graphics accelerators.
- **Reduce:** developer friction in using parallel hardware.
- **Raise:** programmability, libraries, performance per watt, and ecosystem support.
- **Create:** accelerated computing as a platform for AI and scientific workloads.

## Risks and limits

Platform strategy requires sustained developer investment and can face competition from open standards, custom chips, and cloud abstractions.

The case therefore should not be copied mechanically. The transferable insight is the discipline of fit: the offer, channel, operations, revenue model, and cost model must all support the same value curve.

## Transfer lesson

A blue ocean can emerge when hardware is reframed as a programmable platform, expanding the buyer from device purchasers to developers and workload owners.
