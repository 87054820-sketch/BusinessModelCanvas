# Ping An Good Doctor: digital triage before hospital visits

## Context and tension

In China, hospital access can involve long waits, crowded facilities, information asymmetry, and uncertainty about whether an offline visit is necessary. Traditional care concentrates demand at hospitals. Many low-acuity questions need guidance before hospital care.

This is why the case belongs in the Blue Ocean Strategy library. The company did not merely improve within the old rules. It changed which buyers mattered, which factors deserved investment, and which inherited assumptions could be removed. A newcomer should read the case as a market-reconstruction story rather than a normal product-success story.

## The strategic move

Ping An Good Doctor moved triage, consultation, health management, pharmacy links, and insurance ecosystem touchpoints online. It reduced some hospital-front-door friction while raising convenience and continuous access.

The strategic move combines differentiation with a cost or complexity break. The company raises a few factors that matter strongly to overlooked buyers, while reducing or eliminating factors that incumbents treat as mandatory. That asymmetry is what makes the move teachable: the curve is not higher everywhere; it is meaningfully different.

## Read the Business Model Canvas first

Finally, `Cost Structure` and `Revenue Streams` test whether the strategic move can be delivered repeatedly rather than as a one-off campaign.

## Then read the Strategy Canvas

::canvas[blue-ocean-strategy-canvas]{}

The Strategy Canvas should be read as a shape. The red-ocean baseline scores higher on legacy complexity and expertise-heavy factors. Ping An Good Doctor scores higher on access, usability, personalized value, cost efficiency, or new-demand creation, depending on the case. That shape is the strategic argument. If the company tried to score high on every traditional factor and every new factor, it would likely recreate the cost structure of the old industry.

## ERRC logic

- **Eliminate:** the assumption that every health question starts with a hospital visit.
- **Reduce:** waiting, travel, uncertainty, and low-acuity hospital congestion.
- **Raise:** convenience, initial guidance, continuity, and ecosystem linkage.
- **Create:** a digital front door connecting consultation, pharmacy, and insurance.

## Risks and limits

Digital health depends on clinical quality, trust, regulation, reimbursement, and handoff to offline care. It cannot replace hospitals for high-acuity needs.

The case therefore should not be copied mechanically. The transferable insight is the discipline of fit: the offer, channel, operations, revenue model, and cost model must all support the same value curve.

## Transfer lesson

Blue ocean opportunities in healthcare often begin before the formal care episode, where guidance and routing reduce friction for both patients and institutions.
