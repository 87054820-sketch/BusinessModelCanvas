# HealthMedia: digital health coaching between pamphlets and phone counseling

## Context and tension

Health improvement programs often sat at two extremes: generic brochures or websites that were cheap but weak, and human counseling that was effective but expensive and hard to scale. Employers and health plans needed behavior change at population scale.

This is why the case belongs in the Blue Ocean Strategy library. The company did not merely improve within the old rules. It changed which buyers mattered, which factors deserved investment, and which inherited assumptions could be removed. A newcomer should read the case as a market-reconstruction story rather than a normal product-success story.

## The strategic move

HealthMedia used online assessments to personalize digital coaching programs. It reduced generic irrelevance and human labor cost while raising tailored relevance and scalability.

The strategic move combines differentiation with a cost or complexity break. The company raises a few factors that matter strongly to overlooked buyers, while reducing or eliminating factors that incumbents treat as mandatory. That asymmetry is what makes the move teachable: the curve is not higher everywhere; it is meaningfully different.

## Read the Business Model Canvas first

::canvas[business-model-canvas]{}

The BMC turns the value-curve choice into an operating model. In `Customer Segments`, notice that the target is not simply the incumbent industry's favorite customer. In `Value Propositions`, the promise is framed around the new job-to-be-done. In `Key Activities` and `Key Resources`, the model shows what the company must become excellent at. Finally, `Cost Structure` and `Revenue Streams` test whether the strategic move can be delivered repeatedly rather than as a one-off campaign.

## Then read the Strategy Canvas

::canvas[blue-ocean-strategy-canvas]{}

The Strategy Canvas should be read as a shape. The red-ocean baseline scores higher on legacy complexity and expertise-heavy factors. HealthMedia scores higher on access, usability, personalized value, cost efficiency, or new-demand creation, depending on the case. That shape is the strategic argument. If the company tried to score high on every traditional factor and every new factor, it would likely recreate the cost structure of the old industry.

## ERRC logic

- **Eliminate:** one-size-fits-all health advice as the main intervention.
- **Reduce:** dependence on expensive one-to-one counseling.
- **Raise:** personal relevance, scalability, and behavior-change structure.
- **Create:** digital health coaching tailored by assessment data.

## Risks and limits

Digital coaching must prove outcomes, engagement, privacy protection, and clinical credibility. Personalization can become shallow if assessment data is poor.

The case therefore should not be copied mechanically. The transferable insight is the discipline of fit: the offer, channel, operations, revenue model, and cost model must all support the same value curve.

## Transfer lesson

Blue oceans can sit between two strategic groups: combine the effectiveness logic of the premium group with the cost structure of the low-end group.
