# Strategy lenses: Three Horizons and BCG Growth-Share

## Why this case needs more than one portfolio lens

Ping An is already a portfolio-management case, but the portfolio map only says that the group must manage Explore and Exploit units at the same time. The strategic-framework labels become useful only when we separate two questions: **Three Horizons** asks how today's core, emerging engines, and future options migrate over time; **BCG Growth-Share Matrix** asks which units should fund, receive investment, be harvested, or be stopped.

## Three Horizons reading

::canvas[three-horizons-map]{canvasId="d72170c5-5ee8-485d-815f-4a26a2fbffe3"}

H1 is the financial-services core: insurance, banking, wealth management, risk models, distribution, and customer trust. H2 is the set of platform businesses that could become material growth engines if usage frequency and monetization evidence accumulate. H3 is a disciplined option set around data, AI-enabled services, and new ecosystem loops.

The key teaching point is migration. A health or auto platform should not be celebrated simply because it is digital. It moves from H3 to H2 only when evidence shows repeat use, paid demand, supply feasibility, and core synergy. It moves toward H1 only when the group can operate it with a repeatable business model and governance system.

## BCG Growth-Share reading

::canvas[bcg-growth-share-matrix]{canvasId="73d372c6-cd7e-41db-88bd-a6b88ad4f4de"}

The BCG lens treats mature insurance and banking as cash cows that can fund selected growth options. Some digital ecosystems might become stars if market growth and Ping An's relative position both improve. Other pilots remain question marks: high potential, but not yet deserving automatic scale-up.

## What not to overclaim

The BCG matrix does not prove that a unit is attractive by itself. Market share can be ambiguous in platform ecosystems, and regulation can change the economics quickly. Use it as a resource-allocation conversation, then use the portfolio map, BMC, experiments, and evidence scorecard to decide whether the move is robust.
