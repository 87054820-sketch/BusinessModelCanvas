# Reading map — The Art of the Long View

This is a foundation for **Scenario Planning**. It helps teams move from forecasting one future to building several plausible futures and comparing strategic choices across them.

## What problem it solves

- Environment scanning stops at trend lists and does not change strategy.
- Teams assume one most likely future, so the BMC is insensitive to uncertainty.
- Scenarios become optimistic/base/pessimistic forecasts instead of different future logics.
- Strategic moves are not stress-tested across multiple futures.

## Contents / reading map

1. **Scenario mindset** — not predicting the future, but increasing readiness for several plausible futures.
2. **Driving forces** — social, technological, economic, environmental, political, and industry forces.
3. **Critical uncertainties** — variables that would truly change strategic choices.
4. **Scenario stories** — combine uncertainties into distinct, credible future narratives.
5. **Strategic implications** — review BMC, portfolio, capabilities, and experiment priorities under each scenario.
6. **Early signals** — monitor signs that one scenario is becoming more likely.

## How it maps to PinGarden

- `business-model-environment` — collects trends, market forces, industry forces, and macro forces.
- `scenario-matrix` — builds scenarios from two critical uncertainties.
- `business-model-canvas` — tests whether the current model survives different futures.
- `portfolio-map` — compares which businesses or innovation projects are robust.
- `design-criteria-canvas` — turns scenario constraints into design principles.

## What to read first

- For **environment analysis**, start with Driving Forces and avoid collecting buzzwords only.
- For **long-term strategy**, read Critical Uncertainties and Scenario Stories.
- For **portfolio choices**, read Strategic Implications and compare robustness across scenarios.
- For **early warning systems**, read Early Signals and turn scenarios into monitoring lists.

## Common misreadings

- **Scenario planning is not prediction.** It is strategic rehearsal for multiple plausible futures.
- **Good/base/bad is not enough.** Real scenarios have different underlying logics.
- **Scenario stories are not the endpoint.** They must feed back into BMC, portfolio, and experiment priorities.
