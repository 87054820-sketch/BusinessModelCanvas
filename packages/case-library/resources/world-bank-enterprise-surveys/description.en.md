# Reading map — World Bank Enterprise Surveys

World Bank Enterprise Surveys provide firm-level evidence about business environment constraints. They are especially useful when a BMC or Five Forces analysis needs evidence about financing, infrastructure, regulation, corruption, competition, informality, labor, or firm performance.

## What it helps answer

- What constraints do firms in this economy report most often?
- Are financing, infrastructure, or regulation likely to affect the cost structure or key activities?
- Which industry forces are based on real operating friction rather than opinion?
- Which assumptions should become design criteria for market entry?

## How to use with canvases

- `business-model-environment`: convert constraints into industry, market, or macro-force stickies.
- `porters-five-forces`: use firm responses to inform supplier power, buyer friction, rivalry, and entry barriers.
- `business-model-canvas`: attach constraints to key resources, activities, partners, channels, or costs.

## Limitations

Survey coverage varies by country, year, sector, firm size, and formal/informal status. Firm-level survey evidence is stronger than generic opinion, but it still reflects survey design and respondent context.

## Evidence discipline

Use the survey to identify constraints and assumptions to test. Do not treat averages as proof that a specific business can or cannot operate.
