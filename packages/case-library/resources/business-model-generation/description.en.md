# Reading map — Business Model Generation

This is the foundational source for PinGarden's `business-model-canvas`. It explains not only the template, but also why business models should be discussed as visual systems that can be designed, compared, and revised.

## What problem it solves

- Teams use different meanings for “business model”: revenue, product, channel, or operating model.
- BMC is often filled as nine isolated boxes instead of a system of reinforcing choices.
- Case learning stops at “this company succeeded” without extracting reusable model patterns.
- New business work lacks a shared path from observation to prototype, test, and implementation.

## Contents / reading map

1. **Canvas** — the nine building blocks: customer, value, delivery, resources, revenue, and cost as one logic.
2. **Patterns** — reusable structures such as Unbundling, Long Tail, Multi-Sided Platforms, Free, and Open Business Models.
3. **Design** — customer insights, ideation, visual thinking, prototyping, storytelling, and scenario thinking.
4. **Strategy** — business-model environment, SWOT, blue-ocean questions, and portfolio-level thinking.
5. **Process** — mobilize, understand, design, implement, and manage; business-model work is iterative, not one form-filling event.

## How it maps to PinGarden

- `business-model-canvas` — the core system map for how value is created, delivered, and captured.
- `value-proposition-canvas` — expands Customer Segments and Value Proposition when they are still too vague.
- `business-model-environment` — explains why a model works in a specific market context.
- `portfolio-map` — compares multiple models when a company has today's business and tomorrow's options.

## What to read first

- For a **single company case**, start with the nine building blocks and rewrite stickies as mechanisms, not slogans.
- For the **pattern library**, read the Patterns section and connect it to `long-tail`, `multi-sided-platforms`, `free`, `open-business-models`, and `unbundling-business-models`.
- For **new venture design**, read Design and Process; turn ideas into multiple prototypes before committing to one plan.
- For **strategy review**, read Strategy and place the BMC inside environment, blue-ocean, and portfolio questions.

## Common misreadings

- **BMC is not a shorter business plan.** It is a shared hypothesis map that should change as evidence changes.
- **Filling nine boxes is not enough.** The important question is whether choices reinforce each other.
- **Patterns are not recipes.** Long Tail, Free, and Platforms still require validation of customers, supply, network effects, and monetization.
