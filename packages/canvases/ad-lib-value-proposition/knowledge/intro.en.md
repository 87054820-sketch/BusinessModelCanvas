# When to use the Ad-Lib Value Proposition

The Ad-Lib Value Proposition compresses an expanded Value Proposition Canvas into one sentence that can be compared, discussed, and tested. It asks: **who do we create value for, what value do we create, and why should customers choose us instead of the available alternatives?**

It is not final copy. Treat it as a value-proposition hypothesis. A good session produces 3–5 versions, then compares which one is clearest, most differentiated, and most worth testing.

## Reach for it when

- You already have a customer segment, job, pain, gain, and candidate offer, and want to turn them into one sentence.
- You need to compare several value-proposition directions for focus and persuasiveness.
- The team has several competing ways to describe "what problem we solve" and needs a shared expression.
- You are preparing interviews, landing pages, sales scripts, or internal pitches and need a testable statement.

## Skip it when

- You do not yet know who the customer is or what job they are trying to get done — use the Value Proposition Canvas first.
- You only want a polished slogan — validate the value before polishing the words.
- You are trying to squeeze every customer, pain, and gain into one sentence — that will blur the proposition.
