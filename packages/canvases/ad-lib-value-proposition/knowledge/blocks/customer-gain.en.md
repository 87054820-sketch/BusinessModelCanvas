A concrete benefit the customer ends up with: a better outcome, time or cost saving, reduced risk, stronger status, confidence, or positive emotion. Make it measurable or vivid.

## Module Purpose

This slot says what the customer gains when the proposition works. It should describe the customer's desired outcome, not the seller's internal metric.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer is vivid or measurable: "same-day approval with fewer compliance escalations." A weak answer says "better productivity" or "more value" without showing what the customer can feel, see, or measure.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not write gains that sound impressive to the company but are irrelevant to the segment. Also avoid generic business outcomes unless the customer has named them as a success measure.

## Sticky Examples

- "A clean audit trail the CFO can trust before Monday's review."
- "Confidence that the shipment risk was handled before the customer calls."

## Transfer Questions

- What would make the customer say, "This was worth switching for"?
- Is the gain important enough to compete against doing nothing?
- How will you observe or measure the gain in an interview or experiment?

## Source Grounding

The Gain section in Value Proposition Design focuses on outcomes customers expect, desire, or would be surprised by. This module turns that evidence into the positive half of the Ad-Lib.
