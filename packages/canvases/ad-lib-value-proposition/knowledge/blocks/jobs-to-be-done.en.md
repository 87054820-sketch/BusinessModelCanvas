The functional, social, or emotional job the customer hires a solution to do. Phrase it as verb + outcome: "ship a feature on time", not "need a tool".

## Module Purpose

This slot explains the progress the customer wants, independent of your solution. It should translate interview evidence or a JTBD story into a short outcome phrase.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer uses "verb + outcome" and can be true even if your product did not exist. A weak answer describes your feature, such as "use an AI dashboard", instead of the customer's desired progress.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not confuse a task with a feature request. "Export a report" may be a task; "explain performance to the CFO without manual reconciliation" is closer to the job.

## Sticky Examples

- "Close month-end books without chasing five spreadsheets."
- "Choose a vendor without risking a visible procurement mistake."

## Transfer Questions

- What is the customer trying to make possible in their life or work?
- What happens before the job becomes urgent?
- Would the job still matter if your product disappeared?

## Source Grounding

The Jobs / Pains / Gains side of Value Proposition Design and JTBD practice both ask teams to describe customer progress before describing the solution. This Ad-Lib slot preserves that order.
