Choose a verb that explains how you relieve pain: reducing, eliminating, avoiding, lowering. It should connect naturally to the specific customer pain on the right.

## Module Purpose

The verb forces the sentence to say how the offer changes the pain, not merely that the pain exists. It should connect the product/service to a specific Pain Reliever from VPC.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer uses a precise action such as "reduces reconciliation time" or "prevents missed handoffs." A weak answer uses vague verbs like "helps with", "improves", or "solves" without a mechanism.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not overclaim elimination when the product only reduces frequency, severity, or risk. Also avoid claiming pain relief that has not been observed in customer evidence.

## Sticky Examples

- "reducing manual claim follow-up."
- "avoiding late shipment surprises."

## Transfer Questions

- Does the verb describe reduction, avoidance, removal, or risk control?
- Can a customer tell whether this action happened?
- Which pain would remain even after using the offer?

## Source Grounding

Value Proposition Design asks whether products create specific Pain Relievers. This module makes that relationship visible in the one-sentence proposition.
