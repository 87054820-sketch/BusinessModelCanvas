Choose a verb that explains how you create gain: increasing, enabling, accelerating, improving. It should connect naturally to the specific customer gain on the right.

## Module Purpose

The gain verb explains the positive change the offer makes possible. It should point to a Gain Creator from VPC and show whether the value is speed, quality, confidence, status, savings, or reach.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer uses a concrete mechanism: "accelerating approval cycles" or "enabling same-day quote confidence." A weak answer says "improves outcomes" without naming the outcome.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not duplicate the pain verb in positive language. Reducing pain and creating gain are related but not identical; a strong proposition often includes both urgency and aspiration.

## Sticky Examples

- "accelerating supplier quote comparison."
- "increasing confidence before a board presentation."

## Transfer Questions

- What positive outcome does the customer get after the pain is handled?
- Is the gain functional, emotional, social, or financial?
- How would the customer describe success in their own words?

## Source Grounding

Value Proposition Design separates Pain Relievers from Gain Creators so teams can see whether an offer merely removes friction or also creates desired outcomes. This slot carries the Gain Creator into the compressed sentence.
