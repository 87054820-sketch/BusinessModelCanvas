# Falsifiable Hypothesis

Rewrite the risky assumption as a statement that data can validate or disprove. Use TBI's 4-line template:

> **We believe** [specific claim about a customer / channel / value].
> **To verify, we will** [observable action — interview, ad, prototype, sale].
> **And measure** [the metric that answers the claim].
> **We're right if** [pre-set threshold] within [timeframe].

A useful older formula (still works): "We believe `a specific action` will drive `a measurable outcome` within `a timeframe`."

## Check

- Is the action specific and executable?
- Is the result observable and measurable?
- Is the timeframe explicit?
- Is the threshold set BEFORE the experiment runs?
- Is there an outcome that would make you admit the hypothesis is false?

## Examples

- "We believe enterprise CTOs will commit to an annual subscription. To verify, we'll pitch 10 ICP-fit accounts. And measure signed Letters of Intent. We're right if ≥ 3 sign within 4 weeks."
- "We believe fewer form fields increase signup completion. To verify, we'll A/B test 5-field vs 12-field forms. And measure completion rate. We're right if 5-field is +25% within 2 weeks."
- "We believe at least 3 of 5 target customers will try the prototype."
