# Riskiest Assumption

Write the assumption that creates the most risk right now. Per Bland & Osterwalder's TBI, every assumption falls into one of three risk types — **classify yours first**:

- **Desirability** — do customers actually want it? Most early-stage riskiest assumptions live here.
- **Feasibility** — can we build / deliver it? Tech, IP, key resources, partners, capabilities.
- **Viability** — can we earn money from it? Pricing, willingness to pay, unit economics.

Common origins: value proposition, business model, channels, pricing, customer demand, organizational adoption.

## Check

- Which of D / F / V does this fall under?
- If this is wrong, does the idea fail?
- Is it earlier, cheaper, or more necessary to test than other assumptions?
- Is it specific, not just "users will like us"?

## Examples

- "Target customers will pay monthly for automated reports" *(Desirability + Viability)*
- "Procurement owners can finish security review in 7 days" *(Feasibility — channel/operational)*
- "Sales teams will enter leads into the new workflow" *(Desirability — adoption)*
