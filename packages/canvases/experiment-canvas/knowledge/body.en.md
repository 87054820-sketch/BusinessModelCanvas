# Reading the canvas

The Experiment Canvas breaks validation into three moves: define the assumption, design the experiment and metrics, then record results and decide what to do next. This version follows the Design A Better Business structure while incorporating Open Practice Library's emphasis on problem framing, people, metrics, timing, and ownership.

## Recommended order

1. **Riskiest Assumption**: if this is wrong, does the idea fail?
2. **Falsifiable Hypothesis**: write “We believe X will drive Y within Z timeframe.”
3. **Experiment Setup**: choose the method, prototype, sample, timeframe, and owners.
4. **Metrics & Criteria**: define success, failure, and inconclusive thresholds before running.
5. **Results & Conclusion**: record real evidence and decide validated, invalidated, or unclear.
6. **Next Steps**: persevere, pivot, or redesign the experiment.

## What good looks like

- One experiment tests one critical assumption, not everything at once.
- The hypothesis can be proven wrong and has a timeframe plus measurable outcome.
- Metrics are defined before the experiment, not adjusted afterward.
- The sample is sufficient, or the qualitative evidence is clear enough.
- Every next step has an owner and a due date.
