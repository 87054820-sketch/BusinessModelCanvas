# Procurement

The purchasing of inputs, services, and capacity — including raw materials, component vendors, contracted labour, cloud capacity, and rights/licences. The question: what is bought, from whom, and how does sourcing strategy reinforce cost or differentiation?

## Examples

- "Long-term contracts on certified Fair Trade cotton, 3-5 year horizon."
- "Spot-market sourcing for commodity inputs with weekly hedging."
- "Cloud capacity-as-a-service across 2 providers for redundancy and price leverage."

## Flow

Procurement decisions become BMC Key Partners and Cost Structure. A long-term certified-supplier contract is also evidence on the Value Proposition (e.g., "responsibly sourced"). Flag a leak in purple when Procurement and Operations chase opposing goals (e.g., Procurement pushes lowest-cost while Operations needs ultra-consistent quality).
