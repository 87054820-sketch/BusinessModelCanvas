# Market Penetration (市场渗透)

Existing product × existing market: sell more to the customers you already serve. The lowest-risk quadrant. The product is proven, the customer relationship exists, the channels work — the question is whether more demand can be extracted from the same base.

## Core levers

- **Usage frequency** — get existing customers to use more often (consumption nudges, loyalty mechanics).
- **Repeat purchase** — subscription, replenishment, auto-renewal.
- **Share-of-wallet** — bundle adjacent products into the same purchase moment.
- **Win-back** — re-engage lapsed customers.
- **Pricing optimisation** — segmented pricing, tiered plans, price testing.
- **Channel intensity** — more touchpoints with the same customer (mobile + web + email + retail).

## Core Questions

- What is the current customer's frequency, share-of-wallet, churn, and LTV? Where is the gap to industry best-in-class?
- Which of the six levers is the team underusing today?
- What does "win" look like — a 20% lift in repeat purchase, a 15-point share-of-wallet gain?
- Is the existing market saturated, or is there headroom?

## Examples

- "Loyalty program lifting repeat-purchase rate from 35% to 45% for current female-customer base within 12 months."
- "Bundle adjacent SKU into checkout to grow share-of-wallet by ¥12 average order value per cohort."
- "Price-tier introduction to capture price-elastic segment within existing customers without cannibalising premium."

## How it flows into BMC

Market Penetration usually changes BMC **Channels** (new touchpoints), **Customer Relationships** (loyalty / re-engagement programmes), and **Revenue Streams** (new pricing tiers or bundles). Key Activities and Key Resources rarely change much; that is what makes this the low-risk quadrant.
