# How to fill it out

The four forces are written in a particular order on the canvas
(Trends top, Market right, Industry left, Macro-Economic bottom),
but the **scanning order is up to you** — Strategyzer's own guidance
is "scan all four; they reinforce each other." What matters is the
discipline that ties every sticky back to your business model.

## The scanning discipline

For every sticky you drop on this canvas, ask:

1. **Which BMC block does this signal hit?** Customer Segments?
   Cost Structure? Revenue Streams? A trend that doesn't point at a
   block is decoration.
2. **Is this an observed signal, or an aspiration?** The canvas
   collects observations — regulator filings, news, competitor
   moves, search trends, industry reports. Aspirations belong on the
   BMC, not here.
3. **Is it a threat, an opportunity, or a constraint?** Often
   it's all three. Tag accordingly so the team knows what to do with it.

## Suggested fill order

There's no canonical order, but a useful rhythm is:

1. **Key Trends** first — they have the longest fuse and tend to
   cause the other three forces to shift. Technology trends drive
   new entrants; regulatory trends reshape what's allowed; societal
   trends change what customers are willing to do.
2. **Market Forces** — what's actually happening on the demand side.
   This is where shifts in customer needs, segment growth, and
   switching costs live.
3. **Industry Forces** — competitors, new entrants, substitutes,
   suppliers, stakeholders. Read it like Porter's Five Forces with
   stakeholders bolted on. Don't make this the whole scan;
   competitive analysis is one quarter of the picture, not the whole
   picture.
4. **Macro-Economic Forces** — the global backdrop. Capital
   availability, commodity prices, infrastructure quality. Slowest
   to move, but when it moves, it moves everything.

## Common failure modes

- **Only scanning competitors.** The most common mistake. Industry
  Forces is one of four; if every sticky on the canvas is about a
  competitor, the team is doing competitive analysis dressed up as
  environment scanning.
- **Treating the canvas as static.** Environments change. Re-open
  quarterly, even if just to confirm "no change." A canvas filled
  once and shelved is worse than no canvas — it gives the team
  false confidence.
- **Aspirations instead of signals.** "We will be the AI leader" is
  not a Key Trend. "Generative AI is commoditising basic content
  production at 50% lower cost than 18 months ago" is a Key Trend.
- **Single map for several segments.** A B2B enterprise customer
  and a self-serve SMB customer live in different environments. One
  scan each.

## Pairing with the BMC

This canvas is at its most useful when **opened side-by-side with a
Business Model Canvas**. The exercise: read across the BMC's nine
blocks, and for each one ask "which of the four external forces is
applying pressure here, and how?" The pressure points are the
strategic priorities for the next quarter.

The Pairs-with chip in this canvas's inspector links directly to the
BMC. When both canvases live in the same project, you can navigate
between them with one click.
