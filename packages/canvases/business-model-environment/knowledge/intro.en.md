# When to use the Business Model Environment

![Business Model Environment overview — four forces around the BMC](assets/overview.en.png)

The Business Model Environment is Strategyzer's one-page picture of
**what's outside your business model**: four external forces — Key
Trends, Market Forces, Industry Forces, and Macro-Economic Forces —
that bear inward on the nine BMC blocks at the centre. Unlike the
BMC itself, where every block is a thing your team can change,

> *External forces represent design constraints that you have to
> work with.*
> — Strategyzer, *How to scan through your environment's disruptive
> threats and opportunities*

You don't get to redesign the macro economy. You get to scan it,
notice it shifting, and adapt the BMC blocks you *do* control before
the shift breaks them.

The framing the Strategyzer team uses is **proactive** scanning —
the cautionary tales are Kodak (digital photography wasn't a
surprise; the response was) and Blockbuster (Netflix's broadband bet
was visible for years). A canvas like this is meant to be re-opened
quarterly, not filled once and shelved.

## Reach for it when

- You've just finished a fresh **Business Model Canvas** and want a
  paired view of the world it's about to operate in.
- The team disagrees about what "the market" is doing, or what
  "competition" means — different people are scanning different
  forces and don't realise it.
- A previous strategy missed a shift you should have seen coming
  (regulator, technology, substitute product). The next planning
  cycle wants the scan in front of it.
- You're entering a new market or geography and need a quick map of
  the forces that will shape viability there.

## Skip it when

- The decision is operational and short-horizon — pricing a feature,
  fixing a bug. Use a different canvas.
- The team has done no environmental research and doesn't intend to.
  An environment scan with no observed signals is the team writing
  fiction.
- A single canvas is being asked to cover several distinct markets.
  One scan per market segment, or the picture stays vague.

## How this canvas is structured

Four hit-test zones surround a small decorative BMC mini-grid at the
centre, with axis labels (FORESIGHT / MARKET ANALYSIS / COMPETITIVE
ANALYSIS / MACROECONOMICS) hugging the canvas edges to remind you
what kind of analysis each force calls for. The faint sub-category
labels inside each zone (e.g. "Technology · Regulatory · Societal
& cultural · Socioeconomic") are Strategyzer's canonical sub-prompts
— each block's BlockInspector page goes deeper.

Click any of the four zones to open its detail page; click an empty
area to come back here.
