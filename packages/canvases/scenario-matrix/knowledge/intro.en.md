# Scenario Matrix

The Scenario Matrix helps a team rehearse strategy under uncertainty before it locks itself to one forecast. It takes two high-impact, genuinely unresolved uncertainties and turns them into four plausible futures. The goal is not prediction; the goal is better decisions, stronger options, early signals, and robust moves.

## Problem This Solves

Use it when a strategic question is being treated as if there is one likely future, but the real decision depends on external forces nobody controls. The canvas makes the uncertainty visible before the team commits capital, partnerships, product direction, or operating assumptions.

## Beginner Concepts

A **trend** is a force that is likely to continue across most futures. A **critical uncertainty** is a force whose direction or outcome remains unresolved and would materially change strategic choices. A **scenario** is not a forecast, best/worst case, or mood board. It is a coherent story about how a future could work and what it would do to customers, channels, partners, revenue, cost, and competitive behavior.

## When To Use

Use this canvas when external forces make the current BMC fragile: regulation, technology maturity, platform concentration, customer budgets, supply shocks, climate, talent markets, or geopolitical constraints. It is especially useful after Business Model Environment scanning.

## When Not To Use

Do not use it when the team simply needs a short-term sales forecast or a single operating plan. Do not use it to create four decorative stories that never change a decision. If uncertainty is low and the decision is immediate, use a normal plan, risk register, or Experiment Canvas.

## First Step

Start with the decision, not with imagination. Write the decision the team must make, then list the outside forces that could change whether that decision works. Pick two uncertainties only when they are both high-impact and genuinely unresolved.

## Output And Next Step

The output is four plausible futures, a set of early signals to watch, and a short list of robust moves that still make sense across several futures. Feed the strongest implications into Design Criteria Canvas when you need decision standards, or into Portfolio Map when the result changes how much to invest in different bets.
