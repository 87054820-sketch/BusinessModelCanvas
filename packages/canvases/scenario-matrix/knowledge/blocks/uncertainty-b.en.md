# Critical Uncertainty B

The second axis should be independent from the first. If both axes describe the same force, the four futures collapse into repeated stories. Independence gives the matrix its learning value: the team can compare combinations rather than simply ranking optimism.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer creates a meaningful cross with Uncertainty A. A weak answer restates the same market force with different words, such as "AI adoption" and "AI maturity" when they move together.

## Common Pitfalls

- Picking the most exciting topic instead of the most decision-relevant one.
- Creating axes that cannot plausibly combine.
- Choosing an internal execution risk rather than an external uncertainty.

## Sticky Examples

- "Platform market consolidates ↔ platform market fragments."
- "Model performance commoditizes ↔ proprietary capability remains scarce."

## Transfer Questions

- Does each end state combine plausibly with both ends of Uncertainty A?
- Which strategic option becomes attractive only in one quadrant?
