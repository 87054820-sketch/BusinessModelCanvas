# Scenario 3

Scenario 3 is often where teams discover hidden assumptions. Resist making it simply "bad." Ask what new winners, substitutes, constraints, or noncustomers appear when the two axes combine in this way.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer reveals a different route to value or failure. A weak answer only says revenue falls, costs rise, or customers delay.

## Common Pitfalls

- Using the scenario to confirm the team's existing strategy.
- Ignoring noncustomers and adjacent markets.
- Leaving the scenario disconnected from decisions.

## Sticky Examples

- "Restrictive regulation + fragmented platforms: trusted intermediaries become valuable."
- "Budgets grow + commoditized models: differentiation shifts to workflow data and customer success."

## Transfer Questions

- What becomes more valuable here that the current model underinvests in?
- Which experiment could test the most surprising implication?
