# Scenario 2

Scenario 2 should not be a mirror image of Scenario 1. Use it to explore a different strategic logic: who gains power, who loses bargaining leverage, what becomes scarce, and which customer jobs become urgent.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer links external conditions to business-model implications. A weak answer repeats the same recommendation as every other quadrant.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating every quadrant as a market-size estimate.
- Ignoring second-order effects, such as partner behavior or channel cost.
- Writing threats but no opportunities.

## Sticky Examples

- "Open regulation + platform concentration: major platforms become gatekeepers; compliance partnerships matter."
- "Budgets freeze + model quality improves: low-cost automation wins, premium service stalls."

## Transfer Questions

- Which customer segment would you prioritize in this future?
- What option should you preserve even if you do not pursue it now?
