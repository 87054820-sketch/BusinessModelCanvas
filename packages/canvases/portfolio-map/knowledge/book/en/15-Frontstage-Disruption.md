Safaricom is the biggest telecom operator  
in Kenya. In 2007, it decided to use its  
telecom infrastructure to build M-Pesa,  
a simple mobile money transfer system.  
It tapped into the proven demand for mobile 
payments from millions of Kenyans with  
a mobile phone. 
Existing financial services were expensive 
and inappropriate for small transactions. 
In 2009, there were only 352 ATMs and 491 
bank branches in the entire country (with 
a population of 39 million). Most money 
transfers were in cash, which was expensive, 
unreliable, and sometimes dangerous.
M-Pesa changed that. Within two years 
of its introduction, M-Pesa saw 10,000 new 
registrant applications daily.⁶ In 2010, it 
processed over 90% of all mobile money 
transactions in Kenya, and had a 70% mar-
ket share of all mobile money subscribers.⁷
M-Pesa also had an impact on a national 
scale in Kenya with studies crediting M-Pesa 
for lifting an estimated 2% of Kenyan house-
holds out of extreme poverty.⁸
