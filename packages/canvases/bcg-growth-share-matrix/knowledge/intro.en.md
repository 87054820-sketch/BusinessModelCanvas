# When to use the BCG Growth-Share Matrix

Use this canvas when a portfolio discussion is stuck at opinions like "this business is strategic" or "that product still has potential." The matrix forces two questions onto the table: **is the market attractive enough to deserve attention, and do we have enough relative share to win or generate cash?**

It is most useful for comparing business units, brands, product lines, geographies, or categories that can share a data logic. The output is not a label. The output is a portfolio action: invest, selectively test, harvest, reposition, or exit.

## Use it when

- You have multiple businesses or products competing for the same capital, people, or leadership attention.
- Market growth and relative competitive position are the main portfolio questions.
- You can define a comparable market boundary and identify the largest competitor or category leader.
- You need to turn portfolio labels into management actions and BMC follow-up work.

## Do not use it when

- The units are not comparable, such as mixing a company, a feature, and a future venture in the same map.
- You lack even rough data for market growth or relative share.
- Innovation risk, expected return, or disruption trajectory matters more than current market share; use `portfolio-map`, `three-horizons-map`, or `disruption-diagnosis` instead.
- You want an automatic decision rule. A quadrant suggests a conversation, not a final strategy.

## Concepts to know first

**Relative market share** means your share compared with the relevant leader, not just your absolute revenue. **Market growth** should use a consistent market definition; a high-growth niche and a mature broad category can tell different stories. **Cash generation** is an inference from position and economics, so it still needs margin, investment, and business-model evidence.

## First step

Define the portfolio unit and market boundary before placing any sticky. Write one note per unit: what is the market, who is the reference competitor, what data source supports growth, and what data source supports relative share.

## What you should produce

After reading and filling this canvas, you should have a short action list: which units to fund, which Question Marks need evidence before funding, which Cash Cows must be protected, which Dogs require exit or strategic-retention logic, and which units need a full `business-model-canvas` review.

## Next step

Use `portfolio-map` when risk and expected return become the main question. Use `business-model-canvas` before committing resources to any invest, harvest, divest, or reposition decision.
