# Portfolio actions

This block turns quadrant labels into management action. A label without an action does not change the portfolio.

## Examples

- “Invest in Star A, harvest Cash Cow B, divest Dog C.”
- “Run market-share analysis before funding Question Mark D.”

## Flow

Every action should name owner, evidence source, funding implication, and review date.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer turns the matrix into portfolio governance: action, owner, budget, evidence threshold, and review date. A weak answer stops at labels like Star or Dog and never changes resource allocation.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not use the matrix as a static reporting chart. Its value is in cash-flow choices and strategic trade-offs. Also avoid pretending the two axes decide everything; the action should consider synergies, capabilities, regulation, brand, and timing.

## Sticky Examples

- "Question Mark A: fund one distribution experiment; continue only if CAC drops below target by Q3."
- "Cash Cow B: protect service reliability, release 15% of surplus cash to Horizon 2 platform bet."

## Transfer Questions

- Which label requires an immediate investment, harvest, exit, or experiment decision?
- What evidence will change the action next quarter?
- Which decision would free the most scarce resource: cash, talent, attention, or channel capacity?

## Source Grounding

BCG's portfolio logic is not just classification. The matrix connects growth/share position to cash generation, cash use, and resource allocation decisions across the business portfolio.
