# Platform Manual

## Inputs

Bring the platform owner, demand-side participants, supply-side or complementor participants, the value unit, matching or discovery flow, trust mechanisms, pricing model, and current evidence about which side is hardest to activate.

## Output

The output is a map of how the ecosystem creates, delivers, and captures value. A strong map explains the core interaction, where network effects come from, which side needs subsidy or seeding, what governance keeps quality high, and how monetization avoids damaging participation.

## Filling Sequence

1. **Start with participant sides.** Name demand and supply before writing features.
2. **Define the core interaction.** Who creates the value unit, who consumes it, and what filter or matching process connects them?
3. **Map network effects.** Distinguish same-side effects, cross-side effects, negative effects, and multi-homing.
4. **Add governance and trust.** Rules, reviews, identity, quality control, dispute resolution, and incentives are part of the model, not admin detail.
5. **Test monetization tension.** Charging the wrong side, too early, or in the wrong moment can reduce liquidity.
6. **Write cold-start risks.** Decide which side must be seeded first and which non-scalable action creates the first valuable interaction.

## Recommended Cases

Uber and Airbnb are useful for cold start, trust, and multi-sided liquidity. Visa shows payment network effects and merchant/consumer adoption. NVIDIA CUDA shows a developer ecosystem where tools, hardware, and complementary software reinforce each other.

## Related Book Chapters

Read *Platform Revolution* for pipeline versus platform logic, network effects, core interaction architecture, launch strategy, governance, and monetization. The HBR platform strategy article is useful as a professional overview of why platform scale changes competition.

## Next Canvas

Move back to Business Model Canvas to check revenue, cost, partners, and resources. Use Evidence Scorecard when claims about network effects, supply acquisition, or willingness to pay need evidence. Use Value Proposition Canvas for each participant side whose motivation is unclear.

## Common Misreads

- "We have two user groups" does not prove a platform; the interaction must create value.
- Network effects can be negative when more supply lowers quality or more demand creates congestion.
- Monetization is not just revenue design; it changes incentives and may damage the core interaction.
