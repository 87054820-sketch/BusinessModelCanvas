# Demand Side

The demand side is the participant group that wants to consume, buy, search, book, hire, use, or respond to the value unit. Do not call everyone "users." A platform may have riders, travelers, merchants, advertisers, developers, creators, patients, or enterprises, and each has a different job.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer names a specific participant, job, motivation, and success condition. A weak answer says "customers" without explaining why they come to this platform instead of a direct provider or another platform.

## Common Pitfalls

- Mixing demand-side roles that have different jobs.
- Assuming demand will arrive after supply exists.
- Ignoring multi-homing, switching cost, and trust barriers.

## Sticky Examples

- "Travelers: need trustworthy short-stay homes in neighborhoods hotels do not cover."
- "Riders: need a predictable car within 5 minutes at peak commute times."
- "Developers: need distribution, documentation, and revenue share for add-ons."

## Transfer Questions

- What makes this side return after the first interaction?
- Which supply-side improvement would this side notice immediately?
