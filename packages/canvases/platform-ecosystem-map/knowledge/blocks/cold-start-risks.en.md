# Cold Start / Risks

Cold start is the chicken-and-egg problem: demand waits for supply, supply waits for demand. Platform launch strategy often needs seeding, subsidies, piggybacking on an existing network, focusing on a micromarket, using a marquee participant, or temporarily doing manual work.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer names the hardest side to activate, the first market boundary, the non-scalable seeding action, and the risk that could break trust. A weak answer says "growth marketing" without solving liquidity.

## Common Pitfalls

- Launching everywhere instead of winning one dense micromarket.
- Subsidizing both sides with no path to organic value.
- Ignoring fraud, regulation, safety, or backlash until after scale.

## Sticky Examples

- "Start with airport-to-downtown rides; seed drivers during commute peaks."
- "Recruit 50 high-quality hosts in one neighborhood before paid demand acquisition."
- "CUDA: seed developer tools and documentation before expecting third-party ecosystem depth."

## Transfer Questions

- What first interaction would make a participant tell another participant to join?
- Which risk should be monitored with Evidence Scorecard before scaling?
