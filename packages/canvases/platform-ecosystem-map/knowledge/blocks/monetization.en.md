# Monetization

Platform monetization is the design of who pays, when, how much, and for what. It is also governance: pricing changes incentives. A platform may charge transactions, subscriptions, listings, advertising, premium tools, data services, or take rates.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer explains how monetization preserves the core interaction. A weak answer copies a commission model without checking whether it pushes supply or demand away.

## Common Pitfalls

- Charging before liquidity or trust exists.
- Charging the side that creates the scarce value unit.
- Optimizing short-term take rate while weakening network effects.

## Sticky Examples

- "Take rate after completed transaction; no upfront fee for hosts during cold start."
- "Free developer tools, paid enterprise support and certification."
- "Advertisers pay; users keep free access, but ad load must not reduce trust."

## Transfer Questions

- Which side is most price sensitive, and which side receives the most measurable value?
- What monetization test would reveal backlash before a full rollout?
