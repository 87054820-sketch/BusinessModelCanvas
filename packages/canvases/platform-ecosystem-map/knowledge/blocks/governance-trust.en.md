# Governance & Trust

Governance is how the platform sets rules, resolves disputes, curates quality, and protects the core interaction. Trust is not a slogan; it is designed through identity checks, reputation, reviews, guarantees, access rules, API policies, incentives, and enforcement.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer links a governance mechanism to a specific failure mode. A weak answer says "community moderation" or "reviews" without explaining what behavior it changes.

## Common Pitfalls

- Over-governing early and slowing liquidity.
- Under-governing after scale and letting quality collapse.
- Designing rules for the platform owner while ignoring participant fairness.

## Sticky Examples

- "Two-sided reviews reduce rider/driver bad behavior but may need fraud detection."
- "Host guarantee lowers supply fear during cold start."
- "API review protects users but may frustrate developers if criteria are opaque."

## Transfer Questions

- Which trust failure would damage the platform fastest?
- What rule is needed now, and what rule should wait until scale?
