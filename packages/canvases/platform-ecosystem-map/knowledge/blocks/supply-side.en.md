# Supply Side / Complementors

The supply side creates the value unit or makes it more useful. In a marketplace this may be hosts, drivers, sellers, or service providers. In an ecosystem it may be developers, hardware vendors, content creators, agencies, or data partners.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer explains why supply participates, what effort or asset it contributes, and what it expects in return. A weak answer lists providers without understanding their opportunity cost.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating supply as inventory rather than motivated participants.
- Ignoring quality variation and supply constraints.
- Assuming complementors will build before demand exists.

## Sticky Examples

- "Hosts: monetize unused space; need trust, insurance, and calendar control."
- "Drivers: convert idle time into income; need demand density and transparent payout."
- "Developers: build CUDA tools because installed hardware base and documentation lower distribution risk."

## Transfer Questions

- What does this side give up by joining your platform?
- What minimum demand signal would convince supply to participate?
