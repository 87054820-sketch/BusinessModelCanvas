# Won't Haves

These are explicit exclusions and non-negotiable boundaries. They help the team prevent scope creep, wrong incentives, and short-term temptations.

## How to write

- Start with “Won't…” or “Must not…”
- Explain why this is a boundary, not just a temporary lack of resources.
- This area is especially useful for ethics, compliance, brand, privacy, and operational risk constraints.

## Examples

- “Won't rely on heavy manual review workflows”
- “Won't depend on unlicensed data sources”
- “Won't trade privacy for short-term growth”

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good Won't Have states a deliberate boundary and the reason behind it. A weak answer is merely a backlog item postponed because of time or budget.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not use Won't Haves to avoid hard work that is actually necessary. Use them to prevent strategic drift, compliance risk, unethical shortcuts, or scope creep.

## Sticky Examples

- "Won't use customer health data for ad targeting, even if conversion improves."
- "Won't enter a segment that requires bespoke onboarding for every customer."

## Transfer Questions

- What tempting option should the team explicitly avoid?
- Is this boundary strategic, ethical, operational, or brand-related?
- What decision will this prevent later?

## Source Grounding

Design A Better Business treats design criteria as decision rules. Won't Haves make constraints reusable so teams do not re-litigate known bad directions in every concept review.
