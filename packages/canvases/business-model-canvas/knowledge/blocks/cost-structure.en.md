# Cost Structure (成本结构)

The Cost Structure Building Block describes all costs incurred to operate a business model. Creating and delivering value, maintaining Customer Relationships, and generating revenue all incur costs.

Such costs can be calculated relatively easily after defining Key Resources, Key Activities, and Key Partnerships. Some business models, though, are more cost-driven than others.

## Sub-categories / Characteristics

### Cost-Driven

> *Cost-driven business models focus on minimizing costs wherever possible. This approach aims at creating and maintaining the leanest possible Cost Structure, using low price Value Propositions, maximum automation, and extensive outsourcing. No-frills airlines such as Southwest and easyJet are prime examples.*

### Value-Driven

> *Some companies are less concerned with the cost implications of a particular business model design and instead focus on value creation. Premium hotel chains and luxury fashion retailers fall into this category.*

### Fixed Costs

> *Costs that remain the same despite the volume of goods or services produced. Examples include salaries, rents, and physical manufacturing facilities.*

### Variable Costs

> *Costs that vary proportionally with the volume of goods or services produced. Music festivals, for example, have a high proportion of variable costs.*

### Economies of Scale

> *Cost advantages that a business enjoys as its output expands. Larger companies benefit from lower bulk purchase rates, for instance.*

### Economies of Scope

> *Cost advantages that a business enjoys due to a larger scope of operations. For example, a large enterprise can share marketing costs across multiple product lines.*

## Core Questions

- What are the most important costs inherent in our business model?
- Which Key Resources are most expensive?
- Which Key Activities are most expensive?
- Is our model cost-driven or value-driven?

## Examples

- **Fixed Cost** ——「Monthly cloud infrastructure baseline of $12K regardless of traffic」
- **Variable Cost** ——「Per-unit fulfillment cost of $3.50 scaling with order volume」
- **Economies of Scale** ——「Manufacturing unit cost drops 18% at volumes above 10K units/month」

## How it flows into BMC

Cost Structure reveals whether your model can be profitable. It must be weighed against Revenue Streams: the left side of the canvas (activities, resources, partners) drives costs, while the right side (propositions, segments, channels) drives revenue. **A business model where costs consistently exceed revenue is not a business—it's a charity.**
