# Key Activities (关键业务)

The Key Activities Building Block describes the most important things a company must do to make its business model work. Like Key Resources, Key Activities are required to create and offer a Value Proposition, reach markets, maintain Customer Relationships, and earn revenues.

Every business model calls for a number of Key Activities, which are the most important actions a company must take to operate successfully.

## Sub-categories / Types

### Production

> *These activities relate to designing, making, and delivering a product in substantial quantities and/or of superior quality. Production activity dominates the business models of manufacturing firms.*

### Problem Solving

> *This type of activity relates to coming up with new solutions to individual customer problems. The operations of consultancies, hospitals, and other service organizations are typically dominated by problem solving.*

### Platform / Network

> *Business models designed with a platform as a Key Resource are dominated by platform or network related Key Activities. Networks, matchmaking platforms, and software platforms are examples.*

## Core Questions

- What Key Activities do our Value Propositions require?
- Our Distribution Channels?
- Customer Relationships?
- Revenue Streams?
- What activities are most critical to success?

## Examples

- **Production** ——「Design-to-manufacture cycle under 6 weeks for fast fashion」
- **Problem Solving** ——「Custom financial modeling for each client's M&A scenario」
- **Platform** ——「Matching algorithm optimization to reduce rider wait times by 15%」

## How it flows into BMC

Key Activities are the work that turns resources into value. They must directly support the promise made in your Value Proposition. **Activities that don't directly create or deliver customer value are overhead, not Key Activities.**
