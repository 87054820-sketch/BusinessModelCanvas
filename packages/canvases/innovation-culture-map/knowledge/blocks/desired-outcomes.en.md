# Desired outcomes

Desired outcomes are the fruits you want the culture to harvest. They should be more concrete than slogans and observable in quarterly or annual reviews.

Good desired outcomes:
- Have quantity or cadence, e.g. “3–5 critical assumptions validated or invalidated each quarter.”
- Connect to strategy, e.g. “high-potential Explore projects transfer into business units.”
- Allow retirement, e.g. “weak ideas are stopped cheaply.”

Do not write “become innovative.” Write what the innovation culture should reliably produce.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer names a visible result, cadence, and connection to strategy. A weak answer uses aspiration words such as "creative", "agile", or "innovative" without saying what will change in decisions, evidence, or portfolio movement.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not confuse outcomes with activities. "Run more workshops" is a behavior or mechanism; the outcome might be "more validated growth options reach H2." Also avoid outcomes that punish honest learning, such as only rewarding successful launches.

## Sticky Examples

- "Each quarter, two weak Explore projects are retired with documented learning."
- "At least one validated H2 option transfers to a business unit each year."

## Transfer Questions

- What should leadership be able to observe in quarterly reviews?
- Which outcome proves exploration is healthier, not just busier?
- What would be a harmful outcome to avoid?

## Source Grounding

The Invincible Company treats innovation culture as something managed through outcomes, behaviors, and enablers/blockers. Desired outcomes are the "fruits" the culture should consistently produce.
