# Current behaviors

This is often the easiest place to start. Do not write abstract judgments like “we do not innovate” or “people fear failure.” Write observable evidence.

Good sticky format: **who repeatedly does what, in what situation, producing what signal**.

Examples:
- After growth workshops, nobody schedules customer interviews.
- In reviews, leaders ask for three-year revenue forecasts before asking what was recently validated.
- When evidence is bad, teams reframe it as “we need more time.”

Capture both positive and negative behaviors. The Culture Map is not for blame; it is for seeing patterns.
