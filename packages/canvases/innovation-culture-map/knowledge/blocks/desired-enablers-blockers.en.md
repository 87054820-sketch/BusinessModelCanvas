# Enablers to build / blockers to remove

This block turns culture design into leadership actions and organizational mechanisms. Do not write “encourage innovation.” Write which conditions must be created and which blockers must be removed.

Use the three exploration-culture levers highlighted in the book:

1. **Leadership support** — who protects Explore funding? Who asks for evidence rather than theater?
2. **Organizational design** — do Explore projects have separate metrics, funding pools, decision gates, and transfer mechanisms?
3. **Innovation practice** — do teams know how to state assumptions, run small tests, learn, pivot, and retire ideas?

Group mechanisms by time horizon: immediately, within a month, within a quarter, within a year, and practically impossible for now.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer names a concrete leadership action, policy, ritual, metric, capability, or governance change. A weak answer says "support innovation" without specifying which condition will be built or removed.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not add new processes that slow exploration while claiming to support it. Also avoid changing only slogans; culture shifts when mechanisms change the behavior that gets repeated.

## Sticky Examples

- "Create a monthly evidence review where Explore teams can stop weak ideas without penalty."
- "Reserve a small test budget that does not require full annual planning approval."

## Transfer Questions

- Which blocker prevents the highest-value desired behavior?
- What mechanism can leadership change within one quarter?
- How will you know the mechanism is changing behavior rather than creating theater?

## Source Grounding

The Invincible Company frames exploration culture through leadership support, organizational design, and innovation practice. Desired enablers/blockers translate those levers into specific changes.
