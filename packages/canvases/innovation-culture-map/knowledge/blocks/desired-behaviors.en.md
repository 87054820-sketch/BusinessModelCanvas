# Desired behaviors

Desired behaviors are the repeated concrete actions needed to produce desired outcomes. They are not value words.

Ask: if we already had the target innovation culture, what would people do in meetings, reviews, and projects?

Examples:
- Every Explore project states its riskiest assumptions first.
- Teams earn larger resources only after producing evidence through small tests.
- Reviews discuss evidence strength, learning velocity, and risk reduction, not only execution progress.
- Weak ideas are publicly retired and the learning is recorded.

Desired behaviors must be observable, practicable, and rewardable.
