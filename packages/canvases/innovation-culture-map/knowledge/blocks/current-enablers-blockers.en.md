# Current enablers / blockers

Look for the mechanisms that cause current behaviors. The book stresses that enablers and blockers can be formal or informal.

Formal mechanisms: processes, budgets, KPIs, approvals, organization structure, rewards, legal or compliance gates.

Informal mechanisms: leadership preferences, meeting rituals, defaults, fear, knowledge gaps, and trust between teams.

Writing tips:
- Use green stickies for enablers and red stickies for blockers.
- Do not stop at “people are unwilling.” Ask what mechanism makes them act that way.
- Leadership behaviors can also be enablers or blockers.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer identifies the mechanism that makes a behavior repeat: KPI, budget rule, review ritual, incentive, skill gap, or leadership signal. A weak answer says "culture problem" or "people resist change" without naming the condition that produces the behavior.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not stop at personality explanations. If people avoid experiments, ask whether funding gates, legal review, performance metrics, or manager reactions make avoidance rational.

## Sticky Examples

- "Annual bonus tied only to core revenue; Explore learning has no reward."
- "Legal review requires full launch documentation even for small smoke tests."

## Transfer Questions

- What formal rule or informal norm makes this behavior likely?
- Is it an enabler, blocker, or both depending on context?
- Which mechanism would be easiest to change first?

## Source Grounding

The Culture Map treats enablers and blockers as the conditions that shape behavior. The Invincible Company names leadership support, organizational design, and innovation practice as key exploration-culture levers.
