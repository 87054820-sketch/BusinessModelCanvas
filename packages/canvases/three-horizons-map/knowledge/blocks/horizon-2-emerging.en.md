# Horizon 2 — Emerging growth

H2 contains businesses that have evidence and early traction but are not yet reliable core engines.

## Examples

- “Paid pilots show repeated demand, but acquisition is not yet repeatable.”
- “New geography has unit economics, but operations are fragile.”

## Flow

Use `evidence-scorecard` to decide whether to scale, pivot, or pause.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good H2 note names traction, repeatability gaps, capability needs, and the next evidence threshold. A weak answer says "strategic priority" or "growth bet" without showing why it is more mature than H3 and less reliable than H1.

## Common Pitfalls

H2 is the danger zone for organizational tension. It can be killed by H1 metrics too early or scaled before the model is repeatable. Do not move an item to H2 only because leadership likes it.

## Sticky Examples

- "Paid pilots repeat in two verticals; sales cycle still varies from 30 to 120 days."
- "New geography has positive unit economics, but service quality depends on founder involvement."

## Transfer Questions

- What evidence makes this more than an option?
- Which repeatability gap blocks scaling?
- Who should own it when it becomes operational?

## Source Grounding

Three Horizons and The Invincible Company both emphasize the transition problem: emerging businesses need different metrics than the core until evidence and operating ownership are ready.
