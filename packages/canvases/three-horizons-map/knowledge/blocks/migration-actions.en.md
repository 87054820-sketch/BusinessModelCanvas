# Migration actions

This block turns the map into management decisions. A horizon item should move only when evidence, funding, governance, and operating ownership are ready.

## Examples

- “Transfer venture team to operating unit after three repeatable sales cycles.”
- “Keep H3 option alive with small learning budget until cost curve improves.”

## Flow

Every action should name owner, trigger, funding decision, and next review date.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good migration action states the decision, trigger, owner, resource change, and review cadence. A weak answer says "scale later" or "keep exploring" without a condition that would change behavior.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not move items because time passed. Migration is earned by evidence, capability, governance, and market readiness. Also avoid moving a venture into the core before the receiving team can operate it.

## Sticky Examples

- "Move to H2 after three paid renewals in one segment and a named operating owner."
- "Pause H3 if regulation does not advance by Q4; keep one monitoring owner."

## Transfer Questions

- What evidence would trigger migration?
- What budget or team changes when the trigger is met?
- Who has authority to stop, scale, or transfer the item?

## Source Grounding

Three Horizons is useful only when it becomes portfolio governance. McKinsey's framing and The Invincible Company both connect horizon placement to resource allocation, metrics, and organizational handoff.
