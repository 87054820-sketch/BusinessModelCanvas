# Horizon 1 — Core business

H1 contains businesses that create today’s revenue, margin, data, distribution, or operating leverage.

## Examples

- “Core insurance business funds digital health options.”
- “Mature subscription product still has strong retention.”

## Flow

Expand important H1 items into `business-model-canvas` and monitor disruption through `portfolio-map`.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good H1 note names a current business engine, its contribution, and the capability or cash it provides for renewal. A weak answer lists "today's projects" or operational chores that do not explain the core economic engine.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not treat H1 as "this year." H1 is about maturity and reliability. Also do not starve H1 maintenance; a weakening core cannot fund Horizon 2 or Horizon 3.

## Sticky Examples

- "Subscription analytics product: 82% gross retention, funds the healthcare data option."
- "Retail distribution network: high utilization, but vulnerable to direct-to-consumer entrants."

## Transfer Questions

- Which current activity pays for exploration?
- What evidence shows this is still a reliable core rather than a declining legacy?
- What disruption or capability gap should be monitored?

## Source Grounding

McKinsey's three horizons framing treats H1 as the current core that must be extended and defended while funding future growth. The Invincible Company adds the portfolio discipline of managing existing engines alongside exploration.
