# Evidence and risks

This block holds the assumptions that make horizon placement honest. Use it to avoid presenting ambition as fact.

## Examples

- “Willingness to pay is based on interviews, not purchase.”
- “H1 margin depends on a partner contract up for renewal.”

## Flow

Route weak evidence to `evidence-scorecard` and create the next test in `experiment-canvas`.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good risk note distinguishes evidence, assumption, and unknown. It names the decision that depends on the evidence. A weak answer hides uncertainty inside confident language such as "customers will adopt" or "market will mature."

## Common Pitfalls

Do not use the risk box as a generic concern list. Focus on assumptions that would change placement, funding, or migration. Also do not count interviews as purchase evidence unless money or commitment changed hands.

## Sticky Examples

- "Interview interest only; no paid commitment yet. Blocks H2 funding."
- "Partner API access is not contracted; H1 margin forecast depends on renewal."

## Transfer Questions

- Which assumption would make the horizon placement wrong?
- What is the strongest evidence we have, and what is still opinion?
- What experiment or monitoring signal comes next?

## Source Grounding

Testing Business Ideas and Evidence Scorecard practices separate opinion from evidence. The horizon map uses that distinction so ambition does not masquerade as portfolio fact.
