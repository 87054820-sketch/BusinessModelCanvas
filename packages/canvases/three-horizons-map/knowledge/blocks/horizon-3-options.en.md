# Horizon 3 — Future options

H3 contains early options where uncertainty is high and learning matters more than immediate scale.

## Examples

- “AI-native service model depends on adoption behavior we have not observed.”
- “Regulatory change could open a new market, but timing is unclear.”

## Flow

Convert H3 assumptions into `experiment-canvas` tests before asking for scale funding.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good H3 note states the future possibility, the uncertainty, and the learning goal. A weak answer lists fashionable technologies or vague moonshots without a customer, business-model, or timing hypothesis.

## Common Pitfalls

Do not demand H1 financial certainty from H3, but also do not let H3 become a slogan bucket. H3 deserves small learning budgets, explicit assumptions, and kill/continue criteria.

## Sticky Examples

- "Voice-based onboarding for field technicians: adoption unknown; test whether hands-free setup reduces first-week churn."
- "Regulatory data exchange option: market may open if compliance rule passes in 18 months."

## Transfer Questions

- What would make this option valuable if the future changes?
- What is the cheapest learning step?
- What signal would justify moving it toward H2?

## Source Grounding

McKinsey's horizon model places future options in H3, while Testing Business Ideas and Evidence Scorecard logic turn those options into assumptions and experiments rather than speculative plans.
