Three Horizons Map is a portfolio lens for growth over time. It separates what funds the company today, what can become the next growth engine, and what is still an early option.

Use it when the strategic question is not only “which bets are attractive?” but “how do these bets mature, receive evidence, and move between horizons?” Pair it with `portfolio-map` for risk/return position and with `experiment-canvas` / `evidence-scorecard` for de-risking H2 and H3 bets.

## Beginner concept

The three horizons are not calendar years. They are maturity and evidence levels:

- **H1** is reliable enough to run and defend today.
- **H2** has evidence but still needs scaling, ownership, or repeatability.
- **H3** is mostly an option: valuable if the future opens, but still full of assumptions.

A good map shows movement. A sticky is not "in H3 forever"; it should either earn evidence and migrate, stay as a monitored option, or be retired.

## Tiny example

For a software company, the core subscription product may be H1, a paid AI add-on with early renewal evidence may be H2, and a regulated data marketplace idea may be H3. The useful question is not "which one sounds exciting?" It is "which evidence would move the AI add-on into the core, and what cheap signal keeps the marketplace option alive?"
