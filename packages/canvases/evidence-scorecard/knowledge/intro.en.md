# Evidence Scorecard

The Evidence Scorecard helps a team decide what its tests have actually proven. It is for the moment after customer interviews, landing pages, concierge tests, pilots, pre-sales, or sales calls, when the team has activity but still needs a funding, pivot, persevere, or stop decision.

The beginner concept is simple: an opinion is not yet evidence. Evidence becomes useful when it connects a clear assumption, a test design, observable behavior, and a decision. A customer saying "I like it" is weaker than a customer changing workflow, spending time, sharing data, signing a letter of intent, or paying.

## When To Use

Use this canvas when a project has run one or more experiments and the next question is "what do we do now?" It works especially well for Explore teams, startup teams, internal venture boards, and product squads that need to compare weak signals without turning every test into a success story.

## When Not To Use

Do not use it before you can name the critical assumption being tested. If the team is still designing the business model, start with BMC or VPC. If the team has not run any test, use Experiment Canvas first. If the decision is about balancing many ventures, fill this scorecard per venture and then move to Portfolio Map.

## Read These Concepts First

- **Critical assumption:** the belief that would make the idea fail if false.
- **Evidence strength:** how close the test is to real behavior, real commitment, or real economics.
- **Learning velocity:** how quickly tests produce decision-changing insight.
- **Risk reduction:** whether desirability, feasibility, viability, or adaptability risk is lower after the test.
- **Pivot or persevere:** the decision to change direction, continue, add evidence, fund, transfer, or retire.
