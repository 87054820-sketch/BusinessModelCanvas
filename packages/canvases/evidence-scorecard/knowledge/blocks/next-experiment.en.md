# Next Experiment

The next experiment is the cheapest test that can change the decision. It should target the remaining critical risk, use a stronger evidence type when possible, and include pass/fail criteria before the team starts.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer includes hypothesis, method, sample, timebox, evidence type, and decision rule. A weak answer says "do more research" or "build MVP" without explaining what decision will change.

## Common Pitfalls

- Designing the next experiment to confirm what the team already believes.
- Jumping to product build when a manual, smoke, pre-sale, or letter-of-intent test would be faster.
- Forgetting the failure criterion, which makes every result negotiable.

## Sticky Examples

- "Pre-sell to 5 target buyers in 10 days; proceed only if 2 pay a deposit."
- "Wizard-of-Oz delivery for 3 accounts; pass if manual cost stays below 30% of monthly price."
- "Smoke test two channels; stop if qualified lead cost exceeds target by 3x."

## Transfer Questions

- What would make you change your mind?
- Which next canvas will capture the result: Experiment Canvas, BMC, VPC, or Portfolio Map?
