# Risk Reduction

Risk reduction explains which uncertainty has become smaller. Desirability asks whether customers want it. Feasibility asks whether the team can deliver it. Viability asks whether the economics work. Adaptability asks whether the model can survive regulation, partners, or market change.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer is asymmetric: "desirability down, viability unchanged." A weak answer says "risk reduced" without naming the risk type. This matters because strong desirability evidence can coexist with fatal cost or channel risk.

## Common Pitfalls

- Treating customer enthusiasm as proof of revenue.
- Treating technical feasibility as proof of demand.
- Forgetting risks introduced by the experiment itself, such as manual delivery cost.

## Sticky Examples

- "Desirability risk reduced: 9 of 12 target users used workaround today."
- "Viability risk remains: customer acquisition cost not tested."
- "Feasibility risk increased: manual onboarding took 4 hours per account."

## Transfer Questions

- Which risk would make the current BMC fail first?
- What is the cheapest experiment that directly targets the remaining risk?
