# Critical Assumption

This module names the fragile belief being evaluated. A critical assumption is not any open question; it is the belief that can break the project if it is false. Beginners often write a broad goal such as "customers want automation." A stronger version is falsifiable: "finance managers at 50-200 person SaaS companies will pay at least $300 per month to reduce manual reporting time."

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer is specific about customer, job, behavior, threshold, and time. A weak answer is vague, motivational, or rewritten after the evidence appears. Good answers make success and failure criteria possible.

## Common Pitfalls

- Writing several assumptions in one sticky.
- Testing a feature preference when the real risk is willingness to pay.
- Choosing a safe assumption because the team wants a positive result.

## Sticky Examples

- "Clinic admins will schedule 10 patient follow-ups through a manual concierge workflow within two weeks."
- "Two channel partners will introduce us to paying customers without a revenue guarantee."

## Transfer Questions

- If this assumption is false, does your project stop, pivot, or merely change a feature?
- Which BMC block would be hit first: customer segment, value proposition, channel, revenue, or cost?
