# Current Evidence

This module records what has actually happened. Evidence can include observed behavior, usage data, signed commitments, payment, support tickets, workflow screenshots, pilot results, or verbatim customer quotes. Interpretation belongs in later modules; this one should stay close to the source.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer includes method, sample, date, and result. A weak answer compresses everything into a conclusion like "customers loved it." If another team cannot inspect the evidence trail, the sticky is probably too vague.

## Common Pitfalls

- Counting meetings, prototypes, or survey responses as evidence without behavior.
- Mixing team opinion with customer observation.
- Hiding failed tests because they feel embarrassing.

## Sticky Examples

- "Landing page: 1,240 visits from target ads, 74 email signups, 0 paid deposits."
- "Pilot: 3 warehouses used the manual scheduling tool for 14 days; two kept using it after the trial."
- "Quote: 'I already spend Friday afternoons reconciling this by hand.'"

## Transfer Questions

- What would a skeptical investor, product lead, or customer success owner accept as evidence here?
- What evidence is missing because the test design did not expose it?
