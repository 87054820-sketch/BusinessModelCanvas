# Evidence Strength

Evidence strength asks how much weight the team should place on the evidence. In *Testing Business Ideas*, experiments differ by cost, time, and strength. For beginners, the ladder is useful: opinions and stated intent are weak; repeated behavior is stronger; money, switching cost, signed commitment, or real usage in context is strongest.

## Good Answer vs Weak Answer

A good answer explains both the score and the reason. "Medium because users clicked but did not pay" is better than "medium confidence." A weak answer treats confidence as a mood instead of a property of test design.

## Common Pitfalls

- Calling evidence strong because the team wants to continue.
- Treating a large number of weak signals as one strong signal.
- Ignoring whether the sample matches the target customer segment.

## Sticky Examples

- "Weak: 12 interviews showed interest, no behavior observed."
- "Medium: 18 signups from target segment, but no price test."
- "Strong: 5 annual pre-sales signed by budget owners."

## Transfer Questions

- What behavior would move this evidence one level higher?
- Is the evidence strong enough for a product decision, a funding decision, or only a learning decision?
