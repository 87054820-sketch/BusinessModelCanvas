# How to use

1. Choose one project or critical assumption; do not mix several questions on one scorecard.
2. Write current evidence: real behavior, test results, and customer quotes only.
3. Judge evidence strength: weak evidence usually comes from opinions, medium evidence from behavior signals, and strong evidence from payment, commitment, or real usage.
4. Explain whether risk is decreasing and which risks remain untested.
5. Finish with a portfolio decision: persevere, pivot, test more, transfer, fund, or retire.

# Anti-patterns

- Replacing learning with interview counts, meetings, or prototypes.
- Exaggerating weak evidence to win budget.
- Recording only good news and hiding invalidated assumptions.
