# Pains (痛点)

Pains describe anything that annoys your customers before, during, and after trying to get a job done. This includes the obstacles and risks that stand in the way of getting the job done.

Pains can be functional (a solution doesn't work well), social (doing this looks bad), emotional (I feel bad every time I do this), or ancillary (it's annoying to have to go to the warehouse for this).

## Types of Pains

### Undesired Outcomes, Problems, and Characteristics

> *Functional: a solution doesn't work, runs poorly, or has other negative side effects. Social: doing this looks bad or harms reputation. Emotional: I feel bad every time I do this. Ancillary: it's annoying to have to go to the warehouse for this.*

### Obstacles

> *Things that prevent customers from even starting a job or that slow them down. For example: "I don't have the time to get this done precisely" or "I can't afford any of the existing solutions."*

### Risks (Undesired Potential Outcomes)

> *What could go wrong and have significant negative consequences? For example: "When I use this type of solution, I might lose credibility" or "A security breach would be catastrophic for us."*

## Pain Severity

Not all pains are equally intense. Some are extreme, while others are moderate. A good customer profile distinguishes between extreme pains and moderate ones.

## Core Questions

- How do customers define "too expensive"? Too much time, too much money, too much effort?
- What makes your customers feel bad? What frustrates, annoys, or gives them a headache?
- How are current value propositions underperforming? Which features are missing or misused?
- What are the main difficulties and challenges your customers encounter?
- What negative social consequences do your customers fear? Losing face, power, trust, or status?
- What risks do your customers fear? Financial, social, or technical risks?
- What keeps your customers awake at night? What are their biggest issues, concerns, or worries?
- What common mistakes do your customers make? Are they using existing solutions incorrectly?
- What barriers prevent your customers from adopting a value proposition? Upfront investment, steep learning curve?

## Examples

- **Functional** ——「Current CRM requires 8 clicks to log a single sales call」
- **Emotional** ——「Anxious about whether my team can deliver on the deadline」
- **Risk** ——「If this migration fails, we lose 3 days of revenue」
- **Obstacle** ——「No budget allocated for professional design tools this quarter」

## How it flows into VPC

Pains are the friction that stands between the customer and their desired outcome. The stronger the pain, the more valuable a Pain Reliever becomes. **Focus on the most extreme pains first—a value proposition that only solves minor annoyances rarely wins.**
