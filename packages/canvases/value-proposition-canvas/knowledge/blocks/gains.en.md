# Gains (收益)

Gains describe the outcomes and benefits your customers want to achieve. Some gains are required, expected, or desired, while others may surprise them. Gains include functional utility, social gains, positive emotions, and cost savings.

It is important not to confuse gains with pains. Gains are what customers want to achieve; pains are what they want to avoid.

## Types of Gains

### Required Gains

> *Without this gain, the solution wouldn't work at all. For example, the most basic expectation we have of a smartphone is that we can make calls with it.*

### Expected Gains

> *Relatively basic gains that we expect from a solution. For example, since Apple introduced the iPhone, we expect phones to be beautifully designed.*

### Desired Gains

> *Gains that go beyond what we expect but which people really like. When talking to customers, they usually mention these ideas, such as wanting a smartphone to seamlessly integrate with other consumer electronics.*

### Unexpected Gains

> *Gains that go far beyond what customers expect and desire. Even when talking to customers, they wouldn't mention these. Before Apple introduced touch screens as a mainstream feature, nobody thought of touch screens as a phone component.*

## Gain Relevance

Gains can be essential or nice-to-have. Similar to pains, some are extreme while others are moderate.

## Core Questions

- What kind of savings would make your customers happy? Time, money, or effort?
- What quality levels do they desire? Higher or lower?
- How do current value propositions delight your customers? Which features do they love?
- What would make your customers' jobs or lives easier? Easier learning, more services, lower ownership costs?
- What positive social consequences do your customers desire? Looking better, gaining power or status?
- What are customers looking for most? Good design, guarantees, specific features?
- What do customers dream about? What would bring them great pleasure?
- How do customers measure success and failure? How do they assess performance and cost?
- How could you make it easier for customers to adopt a value proposition? Lower cost, less investment, lower risk?

## Examples

- **Required** ——「Must be able to export data to Excel for compliance reporting」
- **Expected** ——「Dashboard loads in under 2 seconds」
- **Desired** ——「AI suggests next actions based on my team's past behavior」
- **Unexpected** ——「Automatically generates a client-ready summary after each meeting」

## How it flows into VPC

Gains represent the upside your customer is chasing. They are the aspirational outcomes that go beyond simply removing pain. **The best value propositions don't just relieve pain—they create unexpected gains that customers didn't even know were possible.**
