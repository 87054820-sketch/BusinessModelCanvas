# Customer Jobs (客户任务)

Customer jobs describe what customers are trying to get done in their work and in their lives. This may be the tasks they are trying to perform and complete, the problems they are trying to solve, or the needs they are trying to satisfy.

When investigating jobs, always analyze them from the customer's perspective. What you think is important from your perspective may not actually be the job the customer is trying to get done.

## Types of Jobs

### Functional Jobs

> *Your customers are trying to perform or complete a specific task or solve a specific problem, such as mowing the lawn, eating healthy, writing a report, or helping customers professionally.*

### Social Jobs

> *Your customers want to look good or gain power or status. These jobs describe how customers want to be perceived by others, such as looking fashionable as a consumer or being perceived as very professional or competent.*

### Personal / Emotional Jobs

> *Your customers seek a specific feeling or emotional state, such as feeling good, feeling secure, or seeking peace of mind as an investor, or gaining a sense of job security in the workplace.*

### Supporting Jobs

> *Customers also do jobs related to buying and consuming value, as value buyers, co-creators, and transferrers. This includes comparing offers, making purchase decisions, taking delivery, and disposing or reselling products.*

## Job Context & Importance

Customer jobs are often performed in a specific context or circumstance that may impose constraints or limitations. For example, calling someone while traveling on a train is different from calling someone while driving.

Not all jobs have the same importance to customers. Some are crucial—failure to complete them leads to serious consequences. Others are less important because customers care more about other things.

## Core Questions

- What is the one thing your customer couldn't live without doing? What is the one thing your customer couldn't live without having?
- What are the main jobs your customer is trying to get done?
- What are the associated jobs that arise before, during, and after getting the main job done?
- In what context are your customers trying to get the job done?

## Examples

- **Functional** ——「Write a monthly performance report for the board」
- **Social** ——「Be perceived as tech-savvy and forward-thinking by peers」
- **Emotional** ——「Feel confident that my retirement savings are secure」
- **Supporting** ——「Compare three vendor quotes and get finance approval」

## How it flows into VPC

Customer Jobs are the anchor of the right side of the canvas. Every Pain and Gain stems from a Job. If you don't understand what the customer is trying to get done, you cannot design a value proposition that matters. **Jobs are the gravity center of value creation.**
