# Say & Do

Public behaviour. The phrases and viewpoints they articulate to peers,
in meetings, in posts — and what they actually do with their time and
attention. In the original Gray canvas these are sometimes split into
two quadrants; here they live together because the **gap between
them** is often the most useful insight on the map.

## What to ask about Say

- What phrases do they repeat in standups, reviews, posts, customer
  calls?
- How do they describe their work to people outside their team?
- What public viewpoint or stance have they taken?
- What do they claim matters to them?

## What to ask about Do

- What do they actually spend their time on?
- What rituals and routines define their week?
- What do they reach for first when a problem appears?
- Where does their calendar disagree with their stated priorities?

## Mind the gap

> "He says he wants speed, but his calendar is 80% governance
> meetings."

The most informative finding on a map is often the **divergence**
between what the person says and what the person does. Capture both
sides on the map (e.g. one sticky for the claim, one for the
behaviour) so the gap is visible at a glance.
