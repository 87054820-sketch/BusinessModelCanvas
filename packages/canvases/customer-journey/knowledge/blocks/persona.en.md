# Persona

The protagonist of this journey. Not a demographic; a **specific
person in a specific situation, with a goal that makes this experience
matter to them**.

A persona band that just says "Small business owner" is the team
ducking the question. A useful one looks more like:

> Maria, 38, Operations Director at a 220-person logistics firm.
> Wants to cut weekly reporting from 6 hours to 1 — her CEO asks for
> the same numbers every Friday, and she's tired of rebuilding the
> spreadsheet by hand.

That gives every later sticky a person to belong to.

## What goes here

- **Name and a one-line situation.** Even a placeholder name is
  better than "the user" — it forces specificity.
- **The goal that makes this journey matter.** Not "use our product"
   — _why are they here in the first place?_
- **The constraint that shapes their behaviour.** Time pressure?
  Budget? A boss watching? The constraint explains the friction.

## What NOT to put here

- Generic demographics ("women aged 25–45"). The journey wants
  one person, not a segment.
- Marketing taglines. The persona is a working artefact, not copy.
- Multiple personas. If you have more than one, duplicate the
  canvas — each persona walks a different journey.

## If you have an Empathy Map

Use it. The persona band is just a one-line summary of the empathy
map's persona/goal anchor — it's the same person you mapped there.
Link the two artefacts; they only work in tandem.
