# BCG Growth-Share Matrix

> A classic portfolio-management framework from BCG that classifies businesses by market growth and relative market share into Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, and Dogs to guide investment, harvesting, selection, and divestment decisions.

# BCG Growth-Share Matrix

## Select this framework when

- The user has a portfolio of comparable businesses, brands, products, or geographies.
- Market growth and relative market share are meaningful allocation lenses.
- The user asks about Cash Cows, Stars, Question Marks, or Dogs.

## Use these canvases

1. `bcg-growth-share-matrix` — place the units and actions.
2. `portfolio-map` — compare with risk/return and Explore/Exploit view.
3. `business-model-environment` — validate market-growth assumptions.
4. `business-model-canvas` — expand high-stakes action decisions.

## Questions to ask

- What is the comparable unit?
- What is the market boundary?
- What is the relative-share proxy?
- Which data supports market-growth assumptions?
- What action follows each quadrant?

## Red flags

Do not draw the matrix without data. Do not fund every Question Mark. Do not harvest Cash Cows until their advantage breaks. Do not keep Dogs without a strategic reason.
